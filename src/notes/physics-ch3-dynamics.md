# Chapter 3: Dynamics

## Key Concepts

1. **Force** — An agent that changes or tends to change the state of rest or uniform motion of a body. It is a vector quantity. SI unit: newton (N).

2. **Inertia** — The natural tendency of a body to resist any change in its state of rest or uniform motion. Greater mass means greater inertia.

3. **Newton's First Law (Law of Inertia)** — A body at rest stays at rest, and a body in uniform motion stays in uniform motion, unless acted upon by an unbalanced external force.

4. **Mass** — The measure of inertia of a body. Larger mass → harder to start moving or stop. SI unit: kilogram (kg).

5. **Newton's Second Law** — When an unbalanced force acts on a body, it produces acceleration in the direction of the force, directly proportional to the force and inversely proportional to the mass. Mathematically: F = ma.

6. **Newton's Third Law** — For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. Action and reaction act on different bodies, so they do not cancel each other.

7. **Momentum** — The quantity of motion possessed by a moving body. It is the product of mass and velocity. Vector quantity. Formula: p = mv. SI unit: kg·m/s or N·s.

8. **Impulse** — The product of force and the time interval for which it acts. It equals the change in momentum. Formula: Impulse = F × t = Δp. SI unit: N·s or kg·m/s.

9. **Law of Conservation of Momentum** — In the absence of an external unbalanced force, the total momentum of an isolated system remains constant. For two bodies: m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂.

10. **Friction** — The force that opposes the relative motion between two surfaces in contact. It acts parallel to the surfaces and opposite to the direction of motion.

11. **Types of Friction:**
    - **Static Friction** — Friction between surfaces at rest relative to each other. Maximum just before motion starts.
    - **Sliding Friction** — Friction when one surface slides over another. It is less than static friction.
    - **Rolling Friction** — Friction when one surface rolls over another. It is the smallest of the three.

12. **Advantages of Friction:**
    - Walking is possible due to friction between shoes and ground.
    - Vehicles can move on roads due to friction between tires and road.
    - Writing on paper is possible due to friction.
    - Nails and screws stay in place due to friction.

13. **Disadvantages of Friction:**
    - Causes wear and tear in machine parts.
    - Produces unwanted heat — engines need cooling.
    - Wastes energy — moving parts need extra force to overcome friction.
    - Slows down moving objects.

14. **Methods of Reducing Friction:**
    - Using lubricants (oil, grease).
    - Using ball bearings or roller bearings.
    - Streamlining shapes to reduce air resistance.
    - Polishing surfaces.

15. **Uniform Motion** — When a body covers equal distances in equal intervals of time. Net force = 0.

16. **Non-Uniform Motion** — When a body covers unequal distances in equal intervals of time. Net force ≠ 0.

## Short Questions

**Q1. State Newton's first law of motion.**

**Ans:** Newton's first law states that a body at rest will remain at rest, and a body in uniform motion will continue its uniform motion in a straight line, unless an unbalanced external force acts on it. This property is called inertia.

**Q2. What is the relation between force, mass, and acceleration?**

**Ans:** According to Newton's second law, the acceleration produced in a body is directly proportional to the applied unbalanced force and inversely proportional to the mass of the body. Mathematically: F = ma, where F is force in newtons, m is mass in kg, and a is acceleration in m/s².

**Q3. Explain Newton's third law with an example.**

**Ans:** Newton's third law states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. Example: When a person jumps from a boat onto the shore, the person pushes the boat backward (action), and the boat pushes the person forward (reaction), enabling the jump.

**Q4. Define momentum and write its formula.**

**Ans:** Momentum is the quantity of motion possessed by a moving body. It is the product of mass and velocity. Formula: p = mv (where p = momentum in kg·m/s, m = mass in kg, v = velocity in m/s). It is a vector quantity whose direction is same as velocity.

**Q5. Define impulse. How is it related to momentum?**

**Ans:** Impulse is the product of force and the time interval during which the force acts. Mathematically: Impulse = F × t. According to the impulse-momentum equation, impulse equals the change in momentum: F × t = Δp = mv_f - mv_i.

**Q6. State the law of conservation of momentum.**

**Ans:** The law of conservation of momentum states that in the absence of an external unbalanced force, the total momentum of an isolated system remains constant. For two colliding bodies: m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂, where u and v are initial and final velocities respectively.

**Q7. Differentiate between static friction and sliding friction.**

**Ans:** Static friction acts between surfaces at rest relative to each other. It is the maximum frictional force and must be overcome to start motion. Sliding friction acts when one surface slides over another. Sliding friction is less than static friction — it takes less force to keep an object moving than to start it moving.

**Q8. List two methods to reduce friction.**

**Ans:**
1. Using lubricants (oil, grease) between surfaces — lubricant fills microscopic irregularities and reduces contact.
2. Using ball bearings or roller bearings — converts sliding friction into rolling friction, which is much smaller.

**Q9. Why does a person fall forward when a moving bus stops suddenly?**

**Ans:** When the bus suddenly stops, the lower part of the person (in contact with the bus) stops with the bus. However, the upper part of the body continues to move forward due to inertia of motion. This causes the person to fall forward.

**Q10. Of a truck and a car moving at the same speed, which has more momentum and why?**

**Ans:** The truck has more momentum. Since momentum p = mv, and both vehicles have the same velocity v, the truck's larger mass gives it greater momentum. This is why it is harder to stop a truck than a car moving at the same speed.

## Long Questions

**Q1. State and explain Newton's laws of motion with examples.**

**Ans:**
**First Law (Law of Inertia):** A body at rest stays at rest, and a body in uniform motion stays in uniform motion, unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.
- Example: A book on a table stays at rest until someone pushes it.
- Example: A passenger lurches forward when a bus suddenly stops — body continues moving due to inertia.

**Second Law:** When an unbalanced force acts on a body, it produces acceleration proportional to the force and inversely proportional to the mass. F = ma (1 N = 1 kg·m/s²).
- Example: Pushing an empty shopping cart is easy (small m, large a). Pushing the same cart full of groceries is harder (large m, small a for same force).

**Third Law:** For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. Action and reaction act on different bodies.
- Example: A rocket pushes exhaust gases downward (action); the gases push the rocket upward (reaction).
- Example: While walking, we push the ground backward; the ground pushes us forward.

**Q2. Explain the concept of friction. Discuss its types, advantages, and disadvantages.**

**Ans:**
**Friction** is a force that opposes relative motion between two surfaces in contact. It always acts parallel to surfaces and opposite to the direction of motion.

**Types:**
1. **Static Friction** — Maximum friction; must be overcome to start motion.
2. **Sliding Friction** — Acts during sliding; smaller than static friction.
3. **Rolling Friction** — Acts during rolling motion; smallest of all three.

**Advantages:**
- Enables walking and running without slipping.
- Allows vehicles to move and brake on roads.
- Makes writing and drawing possible.
- Keeps knots tied and nails fixed in walls.

**Disadvantages:**
- Causes wear and tear of machine parts.
- Produces unwanted heat in machinery.
- Wastes energy — extra fuel needed to overcome friction.
- Slows down moving objects.

**Q3. Derive the relationship between force and momentum. Show that F = ma is a special case of the impulse-momentum equation.**

**Ans:**
Momentum is defined as p = mv.

Rate of change of momentum:
\[
\frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t} = \frac{mv_f - mv_i}{t}
\]
\[
\frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t} = \frac{m(v_f - v_i)}{t}
\]
\[
\frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t} = m \times \frac{v_f - v_i}{t}
\]
\[
\frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t} = m \times a
\]

Newton's second law states that force equals rate of change of momentum:
\[
F = \frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t} = ma
\]

The impulse-momentum equation is F × t = Δp. When we divide both sides by t, we get F = Δp/t = ma. Thus, F = ma is a special case where we consider the instantaneous rate of change of momentum.

**Q4. State and prove the law of conservation of momentum. Give an example.**

**Ans:**
**Statement:** In the absence of an external unbalanced force, the total momentum of an isolated system remains constant. Momentum may be transferred between bodies, but it is never created or destroyed.

**Proof (for two colliding bodies):**
Consider two bodies A and B with masses m₁ and m₂. Let their initial velocities be u₁ and u₂. They collide, and after collision their velocities become v₁ and v₂. Let the collision last for time t.

Force on A due to B:
\[
F_{AB} = \frac{m_1(v_1 - u_1)}{t}
\]

Force on B due to A:
\[
F_{BA} = \frac{m_2(v_2 - u_2)}{t}
\]

By Newton's third law, F_{AB} = -F_{BA}:
\[
\frac{m_1(v_1 - u_1)}{t} = -\frac{m_2(v_2 - u_2)}{t}
\]

\[
m_1(v_1 - u_1) = -m_2(v_2 - u_2)
\]

\[
m_1v_1 - m_1u_1 = -m_2v_2 + m_2u_2
\]

\[
m_1u_1 + m_2u_2 = m_1v_1 + m_2v_2
\]

Total initial momentum = Total final momentum. Hence proved.

**Example:** When a bullet is fired from a gun, the gun recoils backward. Before firing, total momentum = 0. After firing, momentum of bullet (forward) + momentum of gun (backward) = 0.

**Q5. A car of mass 1000 kg moving at 20 m/s collides with a stationary car of mass 800 kg. After collision, they move together. Find their combined velocity.**

**Ans:**
Given: m₁ = 1000 kg, u₁ = 20 m/s, m₂ = 800 kg, u₂ = 0 m/s

By conservation of momentum:
m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = (m₁ + m₂)v

(1000 × 20) + (800 × 0) = (1000 + 800)v

20000 + 0 = 1800v

v = 20000/1800

v = 11.11 m/s

The combined velocity after collision is 11.11 m/s in the direction the first car was moving.

## Numerical Problems

**Q1. A force of 20 N acts on a body of mass 5 kg. Find the acceleration produced.**

**Solution:**
Given: F = 20 N, m = 5 kg
Using F = ma
20 = 5 × a
a = 20/5 = 4 m/s²

---

**Q2. A body of mass 10 kg is moving with a velocity of 15 m/s. Find its momentum.**

**Solution:**
Given: m = 10 kg, v = 15 m/s
p = mv = 10 × 15 = 150 kg·m/s

---

**Q3. A force of 50 N acts on a body for 5 seconds. Find the impulse.**

**Solution:**
Given: F = 50 N, t = 5 s
Impulse = F × t = 50 × 5 = 250 N·s

---

**Q4. A ball of mass 0.5 kg moving at 10 m/s is stopped in 0.02 s. Find the force applied.**

**Solution:**
Given: m = 0.5 kg, v_i = 10 m/s, v_f = 0 m/s, t = 0.02 s
Δp = m(v_f - v_i) = 0.5(0 - 10) = -5 kg·m/s
Using F = Δp/t = -5/0.02 = -250 N
Magnitude of force = 250 N

---

**Q5. A cricket ball of mass 0.16 kg moving at 30 m/s is hit back along the same path at 20 m/s. If contact time is 0.01 s, find the average force exerted.**

**Solution:**
Given: m = 0.16 kg, u = 30 m/s, v = -20 m/s (opposite direction), t = 0.01 s
Change in momentum = m(v - u) = 0.16(-20 - 30) = 0.16(-50) = -8 kg·m/s
F = Δp/t = -8/0.01 = -800 N
Magnitude of average force = 800 N

---

**Q6. A car of mass 1200 kg moving at 18 m/s is brought to rest over a distance of 54 m. Find the braking force and the time taken.**

**Solution:**
Given: m = 1200 kg, v_i = 18 m/s, v_f = 0 m/s, s = 54 m

First find acceleration using 2 a s = v_f² - v_i²
2a(54) = 0² - 18²
108a = -324
a = -3 m/s²

Braking force: F = ma = 1200 × (-3) = -3600 N
Magnitude = 3600 N

Time taken: v_f = v_i + at
0 = 18 + (-3)t
3t = 18
t = 6 s

**Q7. A bullet of mass 20 g is fired from a gun of mass 4 kg with a velocity of 500 m/s. Find the recoil velocity of the gun.**

**Solution:**
Given: m_b = 20 g = 0.02 kg, v_b = 500 m/s, m_g = 4 kg, v_g = ?
Initial momentum = 0 (both at rest)
By conservation of momentum: m_b v_b + m_g v_g = 0
0.02(500) + 4(v_g) = 0
10 + 4 v_g = 0
v_g = -10/4 = -2.5 m/s
Recoil velocity of gun = 2.5 m/s in opposite direction of bullet.

**Q8. A force of 100 N acts on a body at rest of mass 25 kg for 4 seconds. After that, the force ceases to act. Find the distance traveled in 10 seconds from the start.**

**Solution:**
**Phase 1 (with force):** F = 100 N, m = 25 kg, t₁ = 4 s, v_i = 0
a = F/m = 100/25 = 4 m/s²
v_f = v_i + at = 0 + 4(4) = 16 m/s
s₁ = v_i t + ½ a t² = 0 + ½(4)(16) = 32 m

**Phase 2 (no force):** v = 16 m/s (uniform), t₂ = 10 - 4 = 6 s
s₂ = v × t₂ = 16 × 6 = 96 m

**Total distance:** s = s₁ + s₂ = 32 + 96 = 128 m

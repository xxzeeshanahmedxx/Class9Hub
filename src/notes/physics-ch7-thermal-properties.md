# Chapter 7: Thermal Properties of Matter

## Key Concepts

1. **Temperature** — Degree of hotness or coldness of a body measured on a definite scale. SI unit: Kelvin (K).
2. **Heat** — Form of energy that flows from a hotter body to a colder body due to temperature difference. SI unit: Joule (J).
3. **Thermometer** — Device used to measure temperature.
   - **Liquid-in-glass** — Uses expansion of mercury/alcohol. Range: -10°C to 110°C.
   - **Bimetallic** — Uses differential expansion of two bonded metals. Used in thermostats.
   - **Electric/Resistance** — Uses change in electrical resistance with temperature (platinum resistance thermometer). Range: -200°C to 1200°C.
4. **Temperature Scales:**
   - **Celsius (°C):** 0°C (freezing) to 100°C (boiling) of water.
   - **Fahrenheit (°F):** 32°F (freezing) to 212°F (boiling) of water.
   - **Kelvin (K):** 0 K = absolute zero (-273.15°C). No negative temperatures.
5. **Conversion Formulas:**
   - C/100 = (F - 32)/180 = (K - 273)/100
   - K = C + 273
   - F = (9/5)C + 32
6. **Specific Heat Capacity (c)** — Heat required to raise temperature of 1 kg of a substance by 1 K (or 1°C). Q = mcΔT. Unit: Jkg⁻¹K⁻¹.
7. **Thermal Expansion:**
   - **Linear expansion:** ΔL = αL₀ΔT (α = linear expansivity)
   - **Areal expansion:** ΔA = βA₀ΔT (β = 2α for isotropic solids)
   - **Volume expansion:** ΔV = γV₀ΔT (γ = 3α for isotropic solids)
8. **Latent Heat** — Heat absorbed or released during a phase change without temperature change.
   - **Latent heat of fusion (L_f):** Heat to change 1 kg solid → liquid at melting point. Ice: 3.336 × 10⁵ Jkg⁻¹.
   - **Latent heat of vaporization (L_v):** Heat to change 1 kg liquid → gas at boiling point. Water: 2.26 × 10⁶ Jkg⁻¹.
9. **Evaporation** — Vaporization at the surface of a liquid below its boiling point.
10. **Boiling** — Vaporization throughout the bulk of a liquid at a fixed temperature (boiling point).
11. **Heat Transfer:**
    - **Conduction:** Transfer through direct molecular collisions in solids. Rate depends on thermal conductivity k.
    - **Convection:** Transfer by bulk motion of fluids (liquids/gases).
    - **Radiation:** Transfer by electromagnetic waves. No medium required.
12. **Thermal Conductivity (k)** — Measure of a material's ability to conduct heat. Q/t = kA(ΔT/L). Unit: Wm⁻¹K⁻¹.
13. **Greenhouse Effect** — Trapping of infrared radiation by atmospheric gases (CO₂, CH₄, H₂O vapour), warming the Earth's surface.

## Short Questions

**Q1. Differentiate between heat and temperature.**

**Ans:** Heat is the total kinetic energy of molecules in a body, measured in Joules (J). It depends on mass, temperature, and specific heat capacity. Temperature is the average kinetic energy of molecules, measured in Kelvin (K), °C, or °F. It tells how hot or cold a body is.

**Q2. Write the formulas to convert temperature between Celsius, Fahrenheit, and Kelvin scales.**

**Ans:**
- C/100 = (F - 32)/180 = (K - 273)/100
- K = C + 273
- F = (9/5)C + 32
- C = (5/9)(F - 32)

**Q3. Define specific heat capacity. Write its formula and SI unit.**

**Ans:** Specific heat capacity is the amount of heat required to raise the temperature of 1 kg of a substance by 1 K (or 1°C). Formula: c = Q/mΔT. SI unit: Jkg⁻¹K⁻¹.

**Q4. What is thermal expansion? Explain linear and volume expansion.**

**Ans:** Thermal expansion is the increase in size of a material when its temperature increases.
- **Linear expansion:** Increase in length: ΔL = αL₀ΔT, where α is linear expansivity.
- **Volume expansion:** Increase in volume: ΔV = γV₀ΔT, where γ = 3α for isotropic solids.

**Q5. Define latent heat of fusion and vaporization.**

**Ans:** **Latent heat of fusion:** Heat required to change 1 kg of a solid into liquid at its melting point without change in temperature. For ice: 3.336 × 10⁵ Jkg⁻¹.
**Latent heat of vaporization:** Heat required to change 1 kg of a liquid into vapor at its boiling point without change in temperature. For water: 2.26 × 10⁶ Jkg⁻¹.

**Q6. Differentiate between evaporation and boiling.**

**Ans:** **Evaporation:** Occurs at the surface of a liquid at any temperature below boiling point. It is a slow process, causes cooling. **Boiling:** Occurs throughout the bulk of liquid at a fixed temperature (boiling point). It is a rapid process, temperature remains constant during boiling.

**Q7. Explain the three modes of heat transfer with examples.**

**Ans:** 
- **Conduction:** Heat transfer by direct molecular collision in solids. Example: iron rod getting hot at one end when the other is in fire.
- **Convection:** Heat transfer by bulk motion of fluid particles. Example: hot air rising from a heater.
- **Radiation:** Heat transfer by electromagnetic waves without a medium. Example: heat from the Sun reaching Earth.

**Q8. What is the greenhouse effect? Name two greenhouse gases.**

**Ans:** The greenhouse effect is the trapping of infrared (heat) radiation by certain gases in the Earth's atmosphere, which warms the planet. Two main greenhouse gases: Carbon dioxide (CO₂) and Methane (CH₄).

**Q9. Why is water used as a coolant in car engines?**

**Ans:** Water has a high specific heat capacity (4200 Jkg⁻¹K⁻¹), meaning it can absorb a large amount of heat without a large rise in temperature. This makes it an effective coolant for removing excess heat from car engines.

**Q10. What is a bimetallic thermostat? Where is it used?**

**Ans:** A bimetallic thermostat consists of two different metals (e.g., brass and invar) bonded together. When heated, the metals expand by different amounts, causing the strip to bend. This bending makes or breaks an electrical contact, regulating temperature. Used in: electric irons, ovens, room heaters, refrigerators.

## Long Questions

**Q1. Describe different types of thermometers. Compare their working principles, ranges, and uses.**

**Ans:** Three common types of thermometers:

**1. Liquid-in-Glass Thermometer:**
- **Working:** Based on thermal expansion of a liquid (mercury or alcohol) in a glass capillary tube. As temperature rises, liquid expands and rises in the capillary.
- **Range:** -10°C to 110°C (mercury); -115°C to 78°C (alcohol).
- **Uses:** Clinical (body temperature), laboratory experiments.

**2. Bimetallic Thermometer:**
- **Working:** Two different metals with different expansivities (e.g., brass and invar) are bonded together. Temperature change causes differential expansion, bending the strip, which moves a pointer.
- **Range:** -50°C to 500°C.
- **Uses:** Thermostats in irons, ovens, refrigerators; industrial temperature control.

**3. Electric/Resistance Thermometer (Platinum Resistance):**
- **Working:** Based on the principle that electrical resistance of a pure metal (platinum) increases uniformly with temperature. R_T = R₀(1 + αT).
- **Range:** -200°C to 1200°C.
- **Uses:** Accurate industrial measurements, scientific research, very high and very low temperature measurement.

**Q2. Derive the relation between different temperature scales. Convert 40°C, 98.6°F, and 300 K to the other two scales.**

**Ans:** Relation between Celsius (C), Fahrenheit (F), and Kelvin (K):
C/100 = (F - 32)/180 = (K - 273)/100

**(a) 40°C to Fahrenheit & Kelvin:**
F = (9/5)C + 32 = (9/5)(40) + 32 = 72 + 32 = 104°F
K = C + 273 = 40 + 273 = 313 K

**(b) 98.6°F to Celsius & Kelvin:**
C = (5/9)(F - 32) = (5/9)(98.6 - 32) = (5/9)(66.6) = 37°C
K = C + 273 = 37 + 273 = 310 K

**(c) 300 K to Celsius & Fahrenheit:**
C = K - 273 = 300 - 273 = 27°C
F = (9/5)C + 32 = (9/5)(27) + 32 = 48.6 + 32 = 80.6°F

**Q3. Derive expressions for linear, areal, and volume thermal expansion. Give the relation between their coefficients.**

**Ans:** **Linear Expansion:**
When a rod of initial length L₀ is heated by ΔT, its length increases by ΔL.
ΔL ∝ L₀ΔT ⇒ ΔL = αL₀ΔT, where α = coefficient of linear expansion.
Final length L = L₀(1 + αΔT).

**Areal Expansion:**
Consider a square sheet of side L₀, area A₀ = L₀².
After heating: L = L₀(1 + αΔT)
New area A = L² = L₀²(1 + αΔT)² ≈ L₀²(1 + 2αΔT) (neglecting α²ΔT²)
So A = A₀(1 + 2αΔT)
Thus ΔA = 2αL₀²ΔT = βA₀ΔT, where β = 2α = coefficient of areal expansion.

**Volume Expansion:**
Consider a cube of side L₀, volume V₀ = L₀³.
After heating: L = L₀(1 + αΔT)
New volume V = L³ = L₀³(1 + αΔT)³ ≈ L₀³(1 + 3αΔT)
So V = V₀(1 + 3αΔT)
Thus ΔV = 3αV₀ΔT = γV₀ΔT, where γ = 3α = coefficient of volume expansion.

**Relation: β = 2α, γ = 3α (for isotropic solids).**

**Q4. Explain the concept of latent heat. Describe an experiment to determine the latent heat of fusion of ice.**

**Ans:** **Latent heat** is the heat absorbed or released by a substance during a phase change without any change in temperature.

**Experiment to find latent heat of fusion of ice (Method of mixtures):**
1. Take a calorimeter (copper vessel) of known mass m_c and specific heat capacity c_c.
2. Fill it with water of mass m_w at temperature T₁.
3. Take dry ice cubes at 0°C of total mass m_i and quickly add them to the calorimeter water.
4. Stir gently until all ice melts and record the final equilibrium temperature T_f.
5. The ice first melts (latent heat) and then the resulting water warms from 0°C to T_f.

**Heat gained by ice:** Q₁ = m_iL_f + m_ic_w(T_f - 0)

**Heat lost by calorimeter + water:** Q₂ = m_cc_c(T₁ - T_f) + m_wc_w(T₁ - T_f)

By the principle of calorimetry (heat lost = heat gained):
m_cc_c(T₁ - T_f) + m_wc_w(T₁ - T_f) = m_iL_f + m_ic_wT_f

Rearranging:
L_f = [m_cc_c(T₁ - T_f) + m_wc_w(T₁ - T_f) - m_ic_wT_f] / m_i

**Q5. Explain the greenhouse effect. How is it beneficial and what problems does it cause?**

**Ans:** **Greenhouse Effect:**
The Earth's atmosphere contains greenhouse gases (CO₂, CH₄, H₂O vapor, N₂O) that allow short-wavelength solar radiation to pass through but absorb long-wavelength infrared radiation emitted by the Earth's surface. This trapped heat warms the planet, keeping the average temperature around 15°C instead of -18°C.

**Natural (Beneficial):**
1. Maintains Earth's temperature suitable for life.
2. Prevents extreme temperature swings between day and night.
3. Enables agriculture and ecosystem stability.

**Enhanced Greenhouse Effect (Problems from human activities):**
1. **Global warming** — Rise in Earth's average temperature due to increased CO₂ from burning fossil fuels and deforestation.
2. **Climate change** — Altered weather patterns, more frequent storms, floods, and droughts.
3. **Melting glaciers** — Rising sea levels, flooding coastal areas.
4. **Ecosystem disruption** — Loss of biodiversity, coral bleaching.

**Solutions:** Reduce fossil fuel use, plant trees, use renewable energy, improve energy efficiency.

## Numerical Problems

**Q1. Convert 50°C to Fahrenheit and Kelvin.**

**Solution:**
F = (9/5)C + 32 = (9/5)(50) + 32 = 90 + 32 = 122°F
K = C + 273 = 50 + 273 = 323 K

**Q2. How much heat is required to raise the temperature of 2 kg of water from 20°C to 80°C? (c_w = 4200 Jkg⁻¹K⁻¹)**

**Solution:**
Given: m = 2 kg, T₁ = 20°C, T₂ = 80°C, c_w = 4200 Jkg⁻¹K⁻¹
ΔT = 80 - 20 = 60°C = 60 K

Q = mcΔT = 2 × 4200 × 60 = 504000 J = 5.04 × 10⁵ J

**Q3. An iron rod of length 1.5 m at 20°C is heated to 120°C. Find its new length if α for iron is 1.2 × 10⁻⁵ K⁻¹.**

**Solution:**
Given: L₀ = 1.5 m, T₁ = 20°C, T₂ = 120°C, α = 1.2 × 10⁻⁵ K⁻¹
ΔT = 120 - 20 = 100°C = 100 K

ΔL = αL₀ΔT = 1.2 × 10⁻⁵ × 1.5 × 100 = 1.8 × 10⁻³ m = 1.8 mm

New length L = L₀ + ΔL = 1.5 + 0.0018 = 1.5018 m

**Q4. How much heat is needed to completely melt 500 g of ice at 0°C? (L_f = 3.336 × 10⁵ Jkg⁻¹)**

**Solution:**
Given: m = 0.5 kg, L_f = 3.336 × 10⁵ Jkg⁻¹

Q = mL_f = 0.5 × 3.336 × 10⁵ = 1.668 × 10⁵ J

**Q5. A copper calorimeter of mass 100 g (c_c = 390 Jkg⁻¹K⁻¹) contains 150 g of water at 30°C. Ice at 0°C is added and the final temperature is 10°C. Find the mass of ice added. (L_f = 3.336 × 10⁵ Jkg⁻¹, c_w = 4200 Jkg⁻¹K⁻¹)**

**Solution:**
Given: m_c = 0.1 kg, c_c = 390 Jkg⁻¹K⁻¹, m_w = 0.15 kg, c_w = 4200 Jkg⁻¹K⁻¹
T₁ = 30°C, T_f = 10°C, L_f = 3.336 × 10⁵ Jkg⁻¹

Heat lost by calorimeter + water = m_cc_c(30 - 10) + m_wc_w(30 - 10)
= 0.1 × 390 × 20 + 0.15 × 4200 × 20
= 780 + 12600 = 13380 J

Let mass of ice = m_i kg

Heat gained by ice = m_iL_f + m_ic_w(10 - 0)
= m_i(3.336 × 10⁵) + m_i(4200 × 10)
= m_i(333600 + 42000) = m_i × 375600

Heat lost = Heat gained
13380 = m_i × 375600
m_i = 13380 / 375600 = 0.0356 kg = 35.6 g

**Q6. A glass flask has volume 500 cm³ at 20°C. If the coefficient of volume expansion of glass is 2.55 × 10⁻⁵ K⁻¹, find its volume at 80°C.**

**Solution:**
Given: V₀ = 500 cm³, T₁ = 20°C, T₂ = 80°C, γ = 2.55 × 10⁻⁵ K⁻¹
ΔT = 80 - 20 = 60°C = 60 K

ΔV = γV₀ΔT = 2.55 × 10⁻⁵ × 500 × 60
= 2.55 × 10⁻⁵ × 30000 = 0.765 cm³

Volume at 80°C = V₀ + ΔV = 500 + 0.765 = 500.765 cm³

**Q7. A copper rod of area 0.01 m² and length 0.5 m conducts heat. The temperatures at its ends are 100°C and 30°C. If the thermal conductivity of copper is 400 Wm⁻¹K⁻¹, find the rate of heat flow.**

**Solution:**
Given: A = 0.01 m², L = 0.5 m, T₁ = 100°C, T₂ = 30°C, k = 400 Wm⁻¹K⁻¹
ΔT = 100 - 30 = 70°C = 70 K

Rate of heat flow = Q/t = kA(ΔT/L)
= 400 × 0.01 × (70/0.5)
= 400 × 0.01 × 140
= 560 W

**Q8. How much heat energy is required to convert 200 g of water at 100°C into steam at 100°C? (L_v = 2.26 × 10⁶ Jkg⁻¹)**

**Solution:**
Given: m = 0.2 kg, L_v = 2.26 × 10⁶ Jkg⁻¹

Q = mL_v = 0.2 × 2.26 × 10⁶ = 4.52 × 10⁵ J

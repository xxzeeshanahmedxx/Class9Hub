# Chapter 2: Kinematics

## Key Concepts

1. **Rest and Motion** — A body is at rest if its position does not change with time relative to a reference point. A body is in motion if its position changes with time relative to a reference point. Both are relative — the same object can be at rest relative to one observer and in motion relative to another.

2. **Scalars** — Physical quantities that have only magnitude (size). Examples: distance, speed, mass, time, temperature, energy.

3. **Vectors** — Physical quantities that have both magnitude and direction. Examples: displacement, velocity, acceleration, force, momentum.

4. **Distance** — Total path length traveled by a body. It is a scalar quantity. SI unit: metre (m).

5. **Displacement** — Shortest straight-line distance from initial to final position. It is a vector quantity. SI unit: metre (m). Displacement can be zero even when distance traveled is non-zero.

6. **Speed** — Distance traveled per unit time. Scalar quantity. Formula: v = d/t. SI unit: m/s or ms⁻¹.

7. **Velocity** — Displacement per unit time. Vector quantity. Formula: v = s/t. SI unit: m/s or ms⁻¹.

8. **Acceleration** — Rate of change of velocity. Vector quantity. Formula: a = (v_f - v_i)/t. SI unit: m/s² or ms⁻². Positive acceleration means speeding up; negative acceleration (retardation/deceleration) means slowing down.

9. **Uniform Acceleration** — When velocity changes by equal amounts in equal intervals of time.

10. **Equations of Motion** — Three equations for uniformly accelerated motion:
    - First: v_f = v_i + at
    - Second: s = v_i t + ½ a t²
    - Third: 2 a s = v_f² - v_i²

11. **Motion Under Gravity** — When a body falls freely toward Earth, it accelerates uniformly at g = 9.8 m/s² (acceleration due to gravity). Equations become:
    - v_f = v_i + gt
    - h = v_i t + ½ g t²
    - 2 g h = v_f² - v_i²

12. **Distance-Time Graph** — Plots distance on y-axis and time on x-axis. A straight line through origin indicates uniform speed. A curved line indicates non-uniform speed. Parallel to x-axis means body is at rest.

13. **Velocity-Time Graph** — Plots velocity on y-axis and time on x-axis. A straight horizontal line indicates uniform velocity (zero acceleration). A straight sloping line indicates uniform acceleration. Area under the graph gives displacement.

14. **Gravitational Acceleration (g)** — Value differs slightly at different locations on Earth. At sea level near equator: 9.8 m/s². It decreases with altitude and with depth.

15. **Free Fall** — Motion of a body under the sole influence of gravity. Air resistance is neglected in ideal free-fall problems.

## Short Questions

**Q1. Differentiate between distance and displacement.**

**Ans:** Distance is the total path length traveled by a body. It is a scalar quantity and is always positive. Displacement is the shortest straight-line distance from initial to final position. It is a vector quantity and can be zero or negative. Distance ≥ displacement.

**Q2. Define acceleration. What is its S.I. unit?**

**Ans:** Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity of a body. It is a vector quantity. Mathematically, a = (v_f - v_i)/t. Its S.I. unit is metre per second squared (m/s² or ms⁻²).

**Q3. What is meant by uniform acceleration? Give an example.**

**Ans:** Uniform acceleration occurs when a body's velocity changes by equal amounts in equal intervals of time. Example: A freely falling object near Earth's surface accelerates uniformly at 9.8 m/s² (ignoring air resistance).

**Q4. A ball is thrown vertically upward. What is its acceleration at the highest point?**

**Ans:** At the highest point, the velocity of the ball becomes zero momentarily. However, the acceleration due to gravity (g = 9.8 m/s²) still acts downward throughout the motion, including at the highest point.

**Q5. Distinguish between speed and velocity.**

**Ans:** Speed is distance traveled per unit time (scalar), while velocity is displacement per unit time (vector). Speed only tells how fast an object moves; velocity tells both how fast and in which direction.

**Q6. What does the slope of a distance-time graph represent?**

**Ans:** The slope of a distance-time graph represents the speed of the body. A steeper slope indicates higher speed. A horizontal line (zero slope) indicates the body is at rest.

**Q7. Write the three equations of motion for uniformly accelerated linear motion.**

**Ans:**
1. v_f = v_i + at
2. s = v_i t + ½ a t²
3. 2 a s = v_f² - v_i²
Where v_i = initial velocity, v_f = final velocity, a = acceleration, t = time, s = displacement.

**Q8. Define free fall. What is the value of g?**

**Ans:** Free fall is the motion of a body when only the force of gravity acts on it. The acceleration due to gravity (g) is approximately 9.8 m/s² near Earth's surface.

**Q9. Can a body have constant speed but variable velocity? Explain.**

**Ans:** Yes. Velocity is a vector — it changes if direction changes, even if speed stays constant. Example: A car moving around a circular track at constant speed has changing velocity because its direction changes continuously.

**Q10. What is the difference between positive and negative acceleration?**

**Ans:** Positive acceleration (acceleration) occurs when velocity increases with time — the direction of acceleration is same as velocity. Negative acceleration (retardation or deceleration) occurs when velocity decreases with time — the direction of acceleration is opposite to velocity.

## Long Questions

**Q1. Derive the first equation of motion v_f = v_i + at using the definition of acceleration.**

**Ans:**
By definition, acceleration is the rate of change of velocity:

\[
a = \frac{v_f - v_i}{t}
\]

Rearranging:

\[
a \times t = v_f - v_i
\]

\[
v_f = v_i + at
\]

This is the first equation of motion. It relates final velocity (v_f) to initial velocity (v_i), acceleration (a), and time (t).

**Q2. Derive the second equation of motion s = v_i t + ½ a t².**

**Ans:**
For uniformly accelerated motion, average velocity is:

\[
v_{avg} = \frac{v_i + v_f}{2}
\]

Displacement = average velocity × time:

\[
s = \left(\frac{v_i + v_f}{2}\right) \times t
\]

Substitute v_f from the first equation (v_f = v_i + at):

\[
s = \left(\frac{v_i + (v_i + at)}{2}\right) \times t
\]

\[
s = \left(\frac{2v_i + at}{2}\right) \times t
\]

\[
s = v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2
\]

This is the second equation of motion.

**Q3. Derive the third equation of motion 2 a s = v_f² - v_i².**

**Ans:**
From the first equation:

\[
v_f = v_i + at \quad \Rightarrow \quad t = \frac{v_f - v_i}{a}
\]

From the second equation:

\[
s = v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2
\]

Substitute t:

\[
s = v_i \left(\frac{v_f - v_i}{a}\right) + \frac{1}{2} a \left(\frac{v_f - v_i}{a}\right)^2
\]

\[
s = \frac{v_i(v_f - v_i)}{a} + \frac{1}{2} a \cdot \frac{(v_f - v_i)^2}{a^2}
\]

\[
s = \frac{v_i v_f - v_i^2}{a} + \frac{(v_f - v_i)^2}{2a}
\]

\[
s = \frac{2(v_i v_f - v_i^2) + (v_f^2 - 2v_i v_f + v_i^2)}{2a}
\]

\[
s = \frac{2v_i v_f - 2v_i^2 + v_f^2 - 2v_i v_f + v_i^2}{2a}
\]

\[
s = \frac{v_f^2 - v_i^2}{2a}
\]

\[
2 a s = v_f^2 - v_i^2
\]

This is the third equation of motion.

**Q4. Explain distance-time graphs and velocity-time graphs. What information can be obtained from each?**

**Ans:**
**Distance-Time Graph:**
- Plots distance on y-axis and time on x-axis.
- A straight line through origin → uniform speed.
- A curved line → non-uniform speed.
- A line parallel to x-axis → body at rest.
- Slope of graph gives speed.

**Velocity-Time Graph:**
- Plots velocity on y-axis and time on x-axis.
- Horizontal line → uniform velocity (zero acceleration).
- Straight sloping line → uniform acceleration.
- Curved line → non-uniform acceleration.
- Slope gives acceleration.
- Area under the graph gives displacement.
- For a body starting from rest, the graph passes through origin.

**Q5. A ball is thrown vertically upward with an initial velocity of 30 m/s. Calculate: (a) maximum height reached (b) time taken to reach maximum height (c) total time of flight. Take g = 10 m/s².**

**Ans:**
(a) Maximum height:
Using 2 g h = v_f² - v_i²
At max height, v_f = 0
2(-10)h = 0² - 30²
-20h = -900
h = 45 m

(b) Time to reach maximum height:
Using v_f = v_i + gt
0 = 30 + (-10)t
-10t = -30
t = 3 s

(c) Total time of flight:
Time going up = time coming down = 3 s
Total time = 3 + 3 = 6 s

## Numerical Problems

**Q1. A car travels a distance of 200 m in 10 s with uniform speed. Calculate its speed.**

**Solution:**
Given: d = 200 m, t = 10 s
Speed = d/t = 200/10 = 20 m/s

---

**Q2. A bus starts from rest and attains a velocity of 25 m/s in 5 s. Find its acceleration.**

**Solution:**
Given: v_i = 0 m/s, v_f = 25 m/s, t = 5 s
a = (v_f - v_i)/t = (25 - 0)/5 = 5 m/s²

---

**Q3. A cyclist moving at 10 m/s applies brakes and stops after 4 s. Find retardation.**

**Solution:**
Given: v_i = 10 m/s, v_f = 0 m/s, t = 4 s
a = (v_f - v_i)/t = (0 - 10)/4 = -2.5 m/s²
Retardation = 2.5 m/s²

---

**Q4. A train accelerates uniformly from rest at 0.5 m/s² for 20 s. Find the distance covered.**

**Solution:**
Given: v_i = 0 m/s, a = 0.5 m/s², t = 20 s
Using s = v_i t + ½ a t²
s = 0(20) + ½(0.5)(20)²
s = 0 + ½ × 0.5 × 400
s = 0.25 × 400 = 100 m

---

**Q5. A car traveling at 20 m/s accelerates uniformly at 2 m/s² for 10 s. Find its final velocity.**

**Solution:**
Given: v_i = 20 m/s, a = 2 m/s², t = 10 s
Using v_f = v_i + at
v_f = 20 + 2(10)
v_f = 20 + 20 = 40 m/s

---

**Q6. A stone is dropped from a height of 80 m. How long will it take to reach the ground? (g = 10 m/s²)**

**Solution:**
Given: h = 80 m, v_i = 0 m/s, g = 10 m/s²
Using h = v_i t + ½ g t²
80 = 0(t) + ½(10)t²
80 = 5 t²
t² = 16
t = 4 s

---

**Q7. A ball is thrown vertically upward with a velocity of 40 m/s. Find the maximum height reached. (g = 10 m/s²)**

**Solution:**
Given: v_i = 40 m/s, v_f = 0 m/s, g = 10 m/s²
Using 2 g h = v_f² - v_i²
2(-10)h = 0² - 40²
-20h = -1600
h = 80 m

---

**Q8. Using the velocity-time graph method, a body starts from rest and accelerates uniformly at 4 m/s² for 8 s, then moves with uniform velocity for 5 s, and then retards uniformly at 2 m/s² until it stops. Find total distance traveled.**

**Solution:**
**Phase 1 (acceleration):** v_i = 0, a = 4 m/s², t = 8 s
v_f = v_i + at = 0 + 4(8) = 32 m/s
s₁ = v_i t + ½ a t² = 0 + ½(4)(64) = 128 m

**Phase 2 (uniform velocity):** v = 32 m/s, t = 5 s
s₂ = v × t = 32 × 5 = 160 m

**Phase 3 (retardation):** v_i = 32 m/s, v_f = 0, a = -2 m/s²
Using 2 a s = v_f² - v_i²
2(-2)s₃ = 0² - 32²
-4 s₃ = -1024
s₃ = 256 m

**Total distance:** s = s₁ + s₂ + s₃ = 128 + 160 + 256 = 544 m

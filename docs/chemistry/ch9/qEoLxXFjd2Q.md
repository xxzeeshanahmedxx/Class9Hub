# Physical Properties of Metals and Non-Metals — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture steps back from specific groups to provide a broad, comparative overview. The teacher creates a strict comparison between the physical properties of all Metals versus all Non-Metals.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
He reviews macroscopic traits. Metals are generally hard solids with metallic luster (shine). They have high melting and boiling points, are malleable (can be hammered into sheets), ductile (can be drawn into wires), and are excellent conductors of heat and electricity. Non-Metals are the exact opposite: they are dull, brittle if solid, exist often as gases, and are poor conductors (insulators).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Malleability:** The ability of a material to be deformed or hammered into thin sheets without breaking.
- **Ductility:** The ability of a material to be drawn out into a thin wire.
- **Luster:** The shiny appearance of a metal surface reflecting light.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Exception Rule:** Mercury is a metal but is liquid at room temperature.
- **Exception Rule:** Graphite (a non-metal carbon allotrope) is a good conductor of electricity.
- **Exception Rule:** Diamond (a non-metal carbon allotrope) is the hardest naturally occurring substance and an excellent conductor of heat.

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, examiners love testing the exceptions! Always remember that Mercury is the liquid metal, Bromine is the liquid non-metal, and Graphite is the rule-breaking non-metal that conducts electricity.

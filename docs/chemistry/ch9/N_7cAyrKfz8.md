# Properties of Group 1 Elements — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Moving from teh far right to the far left of the table, this lecture covers Group 1: The Alkali Metals. The teacher explains the extreme reactivity and unique physical properties of these elements.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir explains that Alkali Metals (Lithium, Sodium, Potassium, etc.) have only 1 valence electron. They desperately want to lose this electron to achieve a stable octet, making them the most reactive metals. Physically, they are bizarre for metals: they are soft enough to cut with a butter knife and have low densities (some even float on water).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Alkali Metals:** The highly reactive metals of Group 1. They react with water to form strongly alkaline (basic) solutions.
- **Electropositivity:** The tendency to lose electrons, which is extremely high in Group 1.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- They must be stored in oil to prevent them from reacting explosively with moisture or oxygen in the air.
- Reactivity *increases* as you go down the group (Francium is the most reactive).

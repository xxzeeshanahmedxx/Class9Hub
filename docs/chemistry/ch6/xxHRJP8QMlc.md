# Changing the Physical Condition of a Chemical Reaction — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This advanced lecture explores what happens when you disturb a system that is perfectly balanced at dynamic equilibrium. The teacher introduces the concepts behind Le Chatelier's Principle (though the formal principle is usually detailed further in higher classes).

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher explains that Dynamic Equilibrium is fragile. If you change the physical conditions of the closed system—specifically the Concentration, Temperature, or Pressure—the reaction will shift. If you add more reactants, the system speeds up the forward reaction to use them up. If you heat an exothermic reaction, the system shifts in the reverse direction to cool itself down.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Equilibrium Shift:** The changing of the forward or reverse reaction rate in response to an external stress, attempting to restore balance.
- **External Stress:** A change in concentration, pressure, or temperature applied to a system at equilibrium.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Adding Reactants:** Shifts equilibrium Forward (makes more product).
- **Removing Products:** Shifts equilibrium Forward (keeps making product).
- **Increasing Pressure:** Shifts equilibrium to the side with fewer moles of gas.
- **Adding a Catalyst:** Does NOT shift the equilibrium; it only helps the system reach equilibrium faster.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, the Catalyst rule is a massive exam favorite. You guys must remember that a catalyst speeds up BOTH the forward and reverse reactions equally. Therefore, it does absolutely nothing to change the final amounts of products and reactants at equilibrium.

# Dynamic Equilibrium — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the concept of reversible reactions, this lecture introduces the core concept of the chapter: Dynamic Equilibrium. The teacher explains what happens when the forward and reverse reactions happen at the exact same time.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir explains that when a reversible reaction starts, the forward reaction is fast (lots of reactants) and the reverse is zero. As products build up, the reverse reaction speeds up. Eventually, the Rate of the Forward Reaction perfectly equals the Rate of the Reverse Reaction. At this point, the amounts of reactants and products stop changing, even though the reaction is still happening frantically at the microscopic level. This state is Dynamic Equilibrium.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Dynamic Equilibrium:** The state in a reversible reaction where the rate of the forward reaction equals the rate of the reverse reaction.
- **Static Equilibrium:** A state of balance where all reactions have completely stopped (not common in chemistry).
- **Macroscopic Properties:** Visible properties like color, concentration, and density.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- At dynamic equilibrium, the reaction has NOT stopped; it is just moving in both directions at equal speeds.
- Macroscopic properties (like color) remain constant at equilibrium.
- Equilibrium can only be established in a closed system.

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, "Equal Rates, Constant Concentrations." The speed of both reactions must be exactly equal, but the *amounts* of reactants and products do not have to be equal—they just have to stop changing. This is a very common MCQ trap!

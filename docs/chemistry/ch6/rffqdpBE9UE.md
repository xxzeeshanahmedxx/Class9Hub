# Reversible and Irreversible Changes — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Chapter 6 introduces the fascinating concept of Chemical Equilibrium. This first lecture challenges the assumption that chemical reactions only go in one direction, introducing the concept of Reversible Reactions.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir explains that most everyday reactions are Irreversible—like burning a piece of paper; you cannot turn the ash back into paper. However, many chemical reactions in closed systems are Reversible. The products can react with each other to reform the original reactants. The teacher introduces the double arrow symbol ($\rightleftharpoons$) used to represent these two-way reactions.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Irreversible Reaction:** A reaction that proceeds in only one direction (forward) and goes to completion.
- **Reversible Reaction:** A reaction that proceeds in both forward and reverse directions and never goes to completion.
- **Forward Reaction:** Reactants combining to form products.
- **Reverse Reaction:** Products decomposing back into reactants.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Irreversible reactions use a single arrow ($\rightarrow$).
- Reversible reactions use a double half-arrow ($\rightleftharpoons$).
- Example of Reversible: Heating Iodine and Hydrogen in a closed flask forms HI, which then breaks back down into $H_2$ and $I_2$.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, the "closed flask" part is critical. A reversible reaction involving gases can ONLY occur in a closed system. If the flask is open, the gas escapes into the atmosphere, the reverse reaction becomes impossible, and the reaction becomes irreversible by default.

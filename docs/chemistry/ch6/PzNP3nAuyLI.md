## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher reviews the definitions of reversible/irreversible reactions and dynamic equilibrium. He focuses on explaining the graphs that show the forward reaction rate decreasing and the reverse reaction rate increasing until they merge into a single flat line (equilibrium). The teacher also reviews how different external stresses shift specific reactions.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Graphical Representation of Equilibrium:** Using a Rate vs Time graph to visually prove that the forward and reverse rates eventually equalize.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Differentiating between Static (stopped) and Dynamic (moving but balanced) equilibrium.
- Reinforcing that reversible reactions only reach equilibrium in closed flasks.
- Answering MCQs by analyzing how pressure shifts affect gas-phase reactions.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: When drawing the Rate vs Time graph for a long question, ensure the curve for the Forward Reaction starts high (at the y-axis) and goes down, while the Reverse Reaction curve starts at zero (origin) and goes up, until they perfectly merge into a single horizontal line.

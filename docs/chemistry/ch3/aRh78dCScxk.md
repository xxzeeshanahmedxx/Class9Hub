# Nature of Bonding and Properties — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This capstone theoretical lecture ties the entire chapter together. The teacher summarizes how the exact Nature of Bonding (Ionic, Covalent, Metallic) directly dictates the macroscopic physical Properties of the material.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher reviews how atomic structure determines the bond type, which in turn determines the IMFs, which finally dictates whether the substance is a hard solid, a conductive metal, or a volatile gas. The teacher explains the rule "Like Dissolves Like": Polar/Ionic substances dissolve in polar solvents (like water), while non-polar covalent substances dissolve in non-polar solvents (like benzene or petrol).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Like Dissolves Like:** The chemical rule stating that polar solvents dissolve polar solutes, and non-polar solvents dissolve non-polar solutes.
- **Macroscopic Properties:** Properties that can be seen or measured in bulk matter (e.g., melting point, solubility).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Ionic:** High MP/BP, water-soluble, conducts when liquid.
- **Polar Covalent:** Medium MP/BP, water-soluble, non-conductive.
- **Non-Polar Covalent:** Low MP/BP, insoluble in water, non-conductive.
- **Metallic:** Variable MP/BP, insoluble, excellent conductors as solids.

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, if an exam asks "Why does oil not dissolve in water?", you must use the "Like Dissolves Like" rule. Water is highly polar, and oil is non-polar covalent. Because their bonding nature is completely different, they cannot form intermolecular attractions with each other.

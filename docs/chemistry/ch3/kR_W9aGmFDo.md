# Ionic Bond — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the "why" of bonding, this lecture explores the first "how": The Ionic Bond. The teacher explains what happens when metals meet non-metals and the complete transfer of electrons occurs.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher uses the classic example of Sodium Chloride (NaCl). Sodium (a metal) has 1 electron in its outer shell and wants to lose it. Chlorine (a non-metal) has 7 electrons and wants to gain 1. Sodium completely transfers its electron to Chlorine. Sodium becomes a positive ion (cation), and Chlorine becomes a negative ion (anion). The strong electrostatic force that pulls these oppositely charged ions together is the Ionic Bond.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Ionic Bond (Electrovalent Bond):** A chemical bond formed by the complete transfer of one or more electrons from one atom to another.
- **Cation:** A positively charged ion formed by losing electrons.
- **Anion:** A negatively charged ion formed by gaining electrons.
- **Electrostatic Force:** The strong attractive force between oppositely charged particles.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Ionic bonds typically form between Group 1/2 metals and Group 16/17 non-metals.
- They form crystal lattices, not isolated molecules.
- The total charge of the resulting ionic compound is always neutral (zero).

## What Students Should Remember
Look, drawing the Lewis dot and cross structure is crucial here. Board examiners require you to draw the arrows showing the exact movement of the electron from the metal to the non-metal, followed by brackets showing the final charges ($[Na]^+ [Cl]^-$).

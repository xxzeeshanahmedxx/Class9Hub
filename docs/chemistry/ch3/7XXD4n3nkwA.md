# Covalent Bond — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture shifts focus to the Covalent Bond. The teacher explains what happens when two non-metals, both of which *want* to gain electrons, meet each other. Since neither wants to give an electron away, they are forced to share.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher uses Hydrogen ($H_2$) as the primary example. Each hydrogen atom has 1 electron and needs 1 more to complete its duplet. Instead of transferring, they overlap their electron clouds and share the pair of electrons between them. The teacher categorizes covalent bonds into Single, Double, and Triple bonds based on how many electron pairs are shared.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Covalent Bond:** A chemical bond formed by the mutual sharing of valence electrons between two atoms.
- **Single Covalent Bond:** The sharing of ONE pair of electrons (e.g., $H-H$, $Cl-Cl$).
- **Double Covalent Bond:** The sharing of TWO pairs of electrons (e.g., $O=O$).
- **Triple Covalent Bond:** The sharing of THREE pairs of electrons (e.g., $N\equiv N$).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Covalent bonds primarily form between non-metal elements (Groups 13 to 17).
- Shared electrons count toward the octet/duplet of *both* atoms simultaneously.
- Molecules are formed ONLY by covalent bonding (ionic compounds form lattices, not molecules).

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, representing the bonds correctly is the key to full marks. You must remember that a single line ($-$) represents ONE pair (2 electrons), a double line ($=$) represents TWO pairs (4 electrons), and a triple line ($\equiv$) represents THREE pairs (6 electrons).

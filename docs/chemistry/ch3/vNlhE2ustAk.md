# Formation of Covalent Compounds — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the theoretical introduction of the covalent bond, this practical lecture focuses on the actual Formation of Covalent Compounds. The teacher demonstrates how to draw the Lewis structures for complex molecules like Water ($H_2O$), Methane ($CH_4$), and Ammonia ($NH_3$).

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir walks stdents through the "Dot and Cross" method. He shows how the central atom (usually the one requiring the most electrons, like Carbon or Oxygen) is placed in the middle. The teacher emphasizes ensuring every single atom in the final drawing satisfies either the Octet or Duplet rule.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Lewis Dot and Cross Structure:** A diagram that shows the bonding between atoms of a molecule and the lone pairs of electrons that may exist.
- **Lone Pair:** A pair of valence electrons that are not shared with another atom in a covalent bond.
- **Bond Pair:** A pair of electrons shared between two atoms.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- In $H_2O$, Oxygen shares 2 electrons (one with each Hydrogen) and keeps 4 electrons as two non-bonding "Lone Pairs".
- In $CH_4$ (Methane), Carbon shares all 4 of its valence electrons with 4 Hydrogens (no lone pairs).
- In $NH_3$ (Ammonia), Nitrogen shares 3 electrons and keeps 1 lone pair.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: Do not forget the Lone Pairs! When drawing water ($H_2O$), if you only draw the lines to the hydrogens and forget to draw the 4 dots (2 lone pairs) sitting on top of the oxygen atom, the examiner will immediately deduct marks because the octet isn't complete.

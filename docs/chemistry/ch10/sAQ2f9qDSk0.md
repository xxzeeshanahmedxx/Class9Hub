# Global Warming — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Chapter 10 focuses on Environmental Chemistry. This video tackles one of the most pressing issues of our time: Global Warming. The teacher explains the chem behind the Earth's rising temperatures.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir introduces the Greenhouse Effect. The Earth's atmosphere contains greenhouse gases (like Carbon Dioxide and Methane). These gases allow high-energy sunlight to pass through and heat the Earth, but they trap the low-energy infrared radiation (heat) that the Earth tries to reflect back into space. As human activities (burning fossil fuels) increase the concentration of CO₂, more heat is trapped, causing the average global temperature to rise.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Greenhouse Effect:** The trapping of the sun's warmth in the planet's lower atmosphere.
- **Global Warming:** The long-term heating of Earth's climate system due to human activities, primarily fossil fuel combustion.
- **Greenhouse Gases:** Gases like CO₂, CH₄, and water vapor that absorb infrared radiation.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- The greenhouse effect is naturally good; without it, Earth would be freezing.
- *Enhanced* greenhouse effect (due to pollution) is bad.
- Consequences include melting polar ice caps, rising sea levels, and extreme weather.

# Acid Rain — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This video revisits and expands upon the chemistry of Acid Rain, treating it as a major environmental secondary pollutant. The teacher explains the exact chemical pathways that create it in the clouds.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher details how burning coal and oil releases Sulfur Dioxide ($SO_2$) and Nitrogen Dioxide ($NO_2$). In the atmosphere, $SO_2$ oxidizes to $SO_3$, which then reacts with rainwater ($H_2O$) to form highly corrosive Sulfuric Acid ($H_2SO_4$). A similar process creates Nitric Acid ($HNO_3$). The teacher explains how this acidic water damages aquatic ecosystems and degrades limestone buildings.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Acid Rain:** Precipitation containing elevated levels of hydrogen ions (low pH), typically caused by $SO_2$ and $NO_x$ emissions.
- **Leaching:** The process where acid rain washes away essential nutrients from the soil, killing plant life.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Normal rain pH is ~5.6 (due to natural carbonic acid). Acid rain pH is < 5.0.
- Acid rain increases the toxicity of lakes by leaching heavy metals (like Aluminum) from the soil into the water, which kills fish gills.
- It destroys historical monuments made of Calcium Carbonate.

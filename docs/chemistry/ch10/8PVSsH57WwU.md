# Introduction, Composition of Atmosphere, Air Pollutants — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This foundational lecture details the exact chemical makeup of the air we breathe. The teacher covers the Composition of the Atmosphere and categorizes the different types of Air Pollutants.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
He states that clean air is a homogeneous mixture (mostly 78% Nitrogen and 21% Oxygen, with 1% Argon and trace gases). The lecture then introduces Primary Pollutants (gases emitted directly from a source, like $SO_2$ from a factory) and Secondary Pollutants (formed when primary pollutants react with each other or water in the atmosphere, like Sulfuric Acid).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Atmosphere:** The layer of gases surrounding the Earth.
- **Primary Pollutants:** Waste or exhaust products driven directly into the air (e.g., $CO$, $SO_2$, $NO$).
- **Secondary Pollutants:** Pollutants produced by chemical reactions of primary pollutants in the atmosphere (e.g., $H_2SO_4$, Ozone).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Nitrogen is inert and dilutes the highly reactive oxygen to prevent out-of-control fires.
- $CO_2$ is only about 0.04% of the atmosphere, but its increase drives global warming.
- Smog is a secondary pollutant mixture of smoke and fog.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, the difference between primary and secondary pollutants is a guaranteed exam question. Memorize the definitions and at least one example of each: $SO_2$ is primary, but when it turns into $H_2SO_4$ (acid rain), it becomes secondary.

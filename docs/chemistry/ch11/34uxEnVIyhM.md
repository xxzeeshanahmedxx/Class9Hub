# Alkanes — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the introduction, this lecture dives specifically into Alkanes, the family of Saturated Hydrocarbons. The teacher explains their structure, nomenclature (naming rules), and general formula.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher defines Alkanes as hydrocarbons where all carbon atoms are bonded by single covalent bonds. Because they hold the maximum possible number of hydrogen atoms, they are "saturated." The teacher introduces the general formula $C_nH_{2n+2}$ and demonstrates how to build the Homologous Series: Methane ($CH_4$), Ethane ($C_2H_6$), Propane ($C_3H_8$), and Butane ($C_4H_{10}$).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Alkanes (Paraffins):** Saturated hydrocarbons with the general formula $C_n H_{2n+2}$.
- **Homologous Series:** A family of organic compounds with the same general formula, similar chemical properties, and successive members differing by a $-CH_2-$ unit.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- "Paraffin" means "little affinity," reflecting their low chemical reactivity.
- The prefix tells the number of carbons (Meth=1, Eth=2, Prop=3, But=4).
- The suffix "-ane" indicates single bonds only.
- Methane is the main component of natural gas (Sui gas).

## What Students Should Remember
Look, you must memorize the general formula $C_nH_{2n+2}$. Examiners will give you an unknown molecule like $C_5H_{12}$ and ask you to identify its family. By seeing that $12 = (2 	imes 5) + 2$, you can instantly prove it is an alkane (Pentane).

# Preparation of Alkanes — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This practical chem lecture covers the laboratory Preparation of Alkanes. The teacher explains the specific chemical reactions used to synthesize saturated hydrocarbons from other organic precursors.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir details two primary methods for preparing alkanes. The first is the Hydrogenation of Alkenes/Alkynes—adding hydrogen gas ($H_2$) across a double or triple bond using a Nickel or Platinum catalyst at high heat to saturate the molecule. The second method is the Reduction of Alkyl Halides—using Zinc dust and aqueous acid (like $HCl$) to replace a halogen atom (like Chlorine) on a carbon chain with a Hydrogen atom.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Hydrogenation:** The chemical addition of hydrogen to an unsaturated molecule to make it saturated.
- **Reduction:** In organic chemistry, the addition of hydrogen or removal of oxygen/halogens from a molecule.
- **Alkyl Halide:** An alkane where one hydrogen has been replaced by a halogen (e.g., $CH_3Cl$).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Ethene ($C_2H_4$) + $H_2 \xrightarrow{Ni, 250^\circ C}$ Ethane ($C_2H_6$).
- This hydrogenation process is industrially used to convert liquid vegetable oils into solid margarine (Banaspati Ghee).
- Chloromethane ($CH_3Cl$) + $2[H] \xrightarrow{Zn/HCl}$ Methane ($CH_4$) + $HCl$.

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, remembering the specific catalysts is mandatory! For hydrogenation, if you write the equation but forget to write "$Ni, 250^\circ C$" over the arrow, the examiner will deduct marks. The catalyst is what makes the reaction physically possible.

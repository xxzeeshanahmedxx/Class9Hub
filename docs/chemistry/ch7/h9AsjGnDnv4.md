# Acids and Bases — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Chapter 7 introduces Acids and Bases. This opening lecture provides the classical, observational definitions of these two opposing chemical classes, setting the stage for more complex atomic definitions later.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir explains how early chemists identified acids and bases strictly by their physical properties and taste. Acids (from the Latin 'acidus' meaning sour) taste sour and turn blue litmus paper red. Bases (alkalis) taste bitter, feel soapy or slippery to the touch, and turn red litmus paper blue. The teacher also introduces the concept of neutralization.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Acid:** A substance that tastes sour and turns blue litmus red.
- **Base:** A substance that tastes bitter, feels slippery, and turns red litmus blue.
- **Litmus Paper:** An indicator dye extracted from lichens used to test for acidity or basicity.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Examples of everyday acids: Lemon juice (Citric Acid), Vinegar (Acetic Acid), Battery Acid (Sulfuric Acid).
- Examples of everyday bases: Soap, Baking Soda, Drain Cleaner (Sodium Hydroxide).
- Mixing an acid and a base results in a neutral salt and water.

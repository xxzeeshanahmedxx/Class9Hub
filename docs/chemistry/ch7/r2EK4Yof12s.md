# Bronsted - Lowry Concepts of Acids and Bases — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
To fix teh limitations of the Arrhenius theory, this lecture introduces the Brønsted-Lowry Concept. The teacher explains a broader definition of acids and bases that relies purely on the transfer of protons ($H^+$).

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher defines a Brønsted-Lowry Acid as a substance that DONATES a proton ($H^+$) to another substance. A Brønsted-Lowry Base is a substance that ACCEPTS a proton. The teacher demonstrates this with the reaction between Ammonia ($NH_3$) and Water ($H_2O$), showing how water donates a proton to ammonia.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Brønsted-Lowry Acid:** A proton ($H^+$) donor.
- **Brønsted-Lowry Base:** A proton ($H^+$) acceptor.
- **Amphoteric Substance:** A substance that can act as both an acid and a base (e.g., water).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- The $H^+$ ion is literally just a bare proton (since hydrogen lost its only electron).
- In $NH_3 + H_2O \rightarrow NH_4^+ + OH^-$, water is the acid (donor) and ammonia is the base (acceptor).
- This theory works for gases and non-aqueous solutions, fixing the Arrhenius flaw.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, identifying conjugate pairs is critical here. When an acid donates a proton, it becomes a "conjugate base." When a base accepts a proton, it becomes a "conjugate acid." Examiners frequently ask you to label these pairs in a given chemical equation.

# Energetics, Enthalpy — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This highly mathematical lecture introduces the core metric of Chapter 5: Enthalpy. The teacher explains how chemists mathematically quantify the total heat content of a chemical system at a constant pressure.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher tells us that it is impossible to measure the *absolute* total energy of a substance. However, we CAN measure the *change* in energy during a reaction. This total heat content is called Enthalpy ($H$). The teacher introduces the formula for the Change in Enthalpy ($\Delta H$), which is the Enthalpy of the Products minus the Enthalpy of the Reactants.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Enthalpy ($H$):** The total heat content of a system at constant pressure.
- **Change in Enthalpy ($\Delta H$):** The heat absorbed or released during a chemical reaction ($\Delta H = H_{\text{products}} - H_{\text{reactants}}$).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- If $H_{\text{products}}$ is greater than $H_{\text{reactants}}$, $\Delta H$ is positive (heat was absorbed).
- If $H_{\text{products}}$ is less than $H_{\text{reactants}}$, $\Delta H$ is negative (heat was released).
- Enthalpy change is usually measured in kiloJoules per mole (kJ/mol).

## What Students Should Remember
To be fair, Make sure you remember the order of the formula: it is always products minus reactants. if you do reactants minus products, your positive/negative sign will be backwards, and your entire thermodynamic analysis will be incorrect.

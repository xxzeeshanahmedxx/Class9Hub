# Exothermic and Endothermic Reactions Example — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This practical lecture reinforces the theory of heat flow by providing concrete, real-world examples of Exothermic and Endothermic reactions, focusing heavily on how they are written in chemical equations.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir provides the classic exothermic example: the Combustion of Carbon (burning coal). $C + O_2 \rightarrow CO_2$ with a $\Delta H = -393.5 \text{ kJ/mol}$. The teacher then provides an endothermic example: the decomposition of Calcium Carbonate (limestone). $CaCO_3 \rightarrow CaO + CO_2$ with a $\Delta H = +178 \text{ kJ/mol}$. 

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Combustion:** An exothermic chemical reaction between a fuel and oxygen, producing heat and light.
- **Thermal Decomposition:** An endothermic reaction where a single compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances upon heating.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Exothermic Examples:** Burning fuels, respiration, neutralizing an acid with a base.
- **Endothermic Examples:** Photosynthesis, melting ice, baking bread, thermal decomposition.
- In equations, heat can also be written as a product (Exothermic: $+ \text{Heat}$ on right) or a reactant (Endothermic: $+ \text{Heat}$ on left).

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: Memorizing the exact $\Delta H$ value for the combustion of carbon ($-393.5 \text{ kJ/mol}$) is highly recommended, as board examiners frequently ask for one complete, balanced example of an exothermic reaction with its accurate energy value.

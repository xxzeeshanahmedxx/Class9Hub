# Energetics, Exothermic and Endothermic Reactions — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the Enthalpy equation, this lecture categorizes all chemical reactions into two distinct types based on heat flow: Exothermic and Endothermic. 

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher uses the previously defined terms (System and Surroundings). An Exothermic reaction releases heat from the system into the surroundings, making the beaker feel hot. An Endothermic reaction absorbs heat from the surroundings into the system, making the beaker feel cold. The teacher links this back to $\Delta H$.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Exothermic Reaction:** A chemical reaction that releases heat energy to the surroundings ($\Delta H$ is negative).
- **Endothermic Reaction:** A chemical reaction that absorbs heat energy from the surroundings ($\Delta H$ is positive).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Exothermic:** Temperature of surroundings increases. Energy of products < Energy of reactants.
- **Endothermic:** Temperature of surroundings decreases. Energy of products > Energy of reactants.
- The negative sign in $-\Delta H$ literally means energy has *left* the system.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, the $\Delta H$ signs are the most tested MCQs in this chapter. You must memorize: EXothermic = EXit = Negative $\Delta H$. ENdothermic = ENter = Positive $\Delta H$.

# Effect of Temperature on Solubility of Solutes — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the concept of saturation, this lecture focuses on how heat impacts the dissolving process. The teacher explains the direct relationship between thermal energy and a solvent's capacity to absorb solutes.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir explains that increasing the temperature increases the kinetic energy of the solvent molecules. This allows them to break apart the solute particles more effectively. Therefore, for most solid solutes (like sugar or salt), solubility increases as temperature increases. However, the teacher notes that for gaseous solutes (like CO₂ in soda), solubility actually *decreases* when heated.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Solubility:** The maximum amount of solute (in grams) that can be dissolved in 100 grams of solvent at a specific temperature to form a saturated solution.
- **Kinetic Energy:** The energy of motion, which increases with heat, allowing solvent molecules to collide more forcefully with solute particles.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Solids in Liquids:** Heating usually increases solubility (e.g., hot tea dissolves sugar faster than iced tea).
- **Gases in Liquids:** Heating decreases solubility (e.g., warm soda goes flat because the gas escapes).
- Cooling a hot saturated solution forces the excess solute to solidify, creating crystals.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, the gas exception is a favorite board exam trick. While heating helps dissolve solids, heating a liquid makes it *harder* to keep gases dissolved. This is why aquatic life struggles in unusually warm lake water—the dissolved oxygen escapes!

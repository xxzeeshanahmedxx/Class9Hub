## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Conceptual Synthesis:** Applying the rules of atomic numbers, isotopes, and subatomic properties to solve specific textbook problems.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Reaffirming the formula: Neutrons = Mass Number - Atomic Number.
- Reviewing the applications of radioisotopes (Iodine-131, C-14).
- Ensuring students can draw the shell structure (K, L, M) according to Bohr's model.
- Highlighting that chemical properties depend on outer-shell electrons, not the nucleus.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: When asked to compare Rutherford and Bohr, always make a T-chart. Focus on the core difference: Rutherford suggested continuous spiraling orbits, while Bohr proved orbits are fixed (quantized) energy levels. Highlighting "Quantized Energy" secures full marks.

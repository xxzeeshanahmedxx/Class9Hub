# Structure of Atom, Discovery of Electron — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Chapter 2 plunges into the microscopic world. This lecture covers the Structure of the Atom, specifically detailing J.J. Thomson's famous Cathode Ray Tube experiment that led to the discovery of the first subatomic particle: the electron.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher explains that atoms were once thought to be indivisible solid spheres. He then describes the discharge tube experiment, where high voltage applied to a gas at low pressure produced glowing "cathode rays". Since these rays were attracted to a positive magnetic plate, scientists concluded they were made of negatively charged particles—later named electrons.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Atom:** The smallest particle of an element that can take part in a chemical reaction.
- **Cathode Rays:** Streams of electrons observed in vacuum tubes.
- **Electron:** A negatively charged subatomic particle discovered by J.J. Thomson.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Cathode rays travel in straight lines from the cathode (-) to the anode (+).
- They cast a sharp shadow of opaque objects, proving they travel in straight paths.
- They are deflected by electric and magnetic fields, proving they have a negative charge.
- The nature of the rays is independent of the gas used in the tube.

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, You guys must remember the properties of cathode rays perfectly. the fact that the rays are the same regardless of which gas is put inside the tube was the ultimate proof that electrons are a fundamental building block of *all* matter.

# Discovery of Nucleus & Bohr's Atomic Model — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lesson covers two monumental leaps in atomic theory: Rutherford's Gold Foil Experiment (which discovered the nucleus) and Bohr's subsequent Atomic Model (which explained electron orbits).

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir explains Rutherford's experiment, where alpha particles were fired at a thin gold foil. While most passed straight through, a few bounced back, proving the existence of a dense, tiny, positive center called the Nucleus. Then, the teacher introduces Bohr's Model, which fixed the flaws in Rutherford's theory by proposing that electrons orbit the nucleus in fixed, quantized circular paths (shells) without losing energy.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Nucleus:** The dense, positively charged central core of an atom.
- **Alpha Particles:** Positively charged helium nuclei emitted by radioactive substances.
- **Orbits (Shells):** Fixed circular paths around the nucleus where electrons revolve with a specific amount of energy.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Rutherford's Conclusion:** The atom is mostly empty space, but contains a heavy, positive nucleus.
- **Bohr's Postulates:** Electrons revolve in fixed orbits.
- Electrons only absorb or emit energy when they jump from one orbit to another.
- $E_2 - E_1 = h\nu$ (Planck's equation for energy difference).

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, knowing the defects of Rutherford's model is just as important as knowing Bohr's model. Rutherford couldn't explain why the negative electrons didn't just spiral into the positive nucleus and collapse the atom. Bohr solved this by introducing "quantized" (fixed) energy levels.

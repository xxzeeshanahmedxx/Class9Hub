# Discovery of Proton and Neutron — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture steps back into history to cover the discovery of the remaining two subatomic particles: the Proton and the Neutron. The teacher explains the experiments that completed our understanding of the atomic nucleus.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir describes Eugen Goldstein's discovery of positive rays (canal rays) in the discharge tube, which were later identified by Rutherford as Protons. Later, since the mass of protons and electrons couldn't account for the total mass of the atom, scientists knew something was missing. The teacher explains James Chadwick's 1932 experiment, where he bombarded Beryllium with alpha particles to discover the neutral, heavy particle: the Neutron.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Proton:** A positively charged subatomic particle found in the nucleus.
- **Canal Rays:** Streams of positively charged ions produced in a discharge tube.
- **Neutron:** A neutral (uncharged) subatomic particle found in the nucleus, possessing a mass almost equal to a proton.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Canal rays travel in the opposite direction of cathode rays.
- The mass of a proton is roughly 1836 times heavier than an electron.
- Neutrons carry no electrical charge, making them highly penetrating.
- Neutrons and Protons make up 99.9% of an atom's mass.

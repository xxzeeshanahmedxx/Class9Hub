# Application of Radio Active Isotopes — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the introduction to isotopes, this lecture explores their real-world utility. The teacher explains the Applications of Radioactive Isotopes in medicine, agriculture, and power generation.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher tells us that unstable isotopes (radioisotopes) emit radiation as they decay. Scientists harness this radiation for various uses. The teacher highlights medical applications like using Iodine-131 to diagnose thyroid issues and Cobalt-60 to treat cancer. The lecture also covers archaeological carbon dating and tracing biochemical pathways in plants.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Radioisotopes:** Unstable isotopes that emit radioactive radiation.
- **Radiotherapy:** The medical use of radiation (from isotopes like Co-60) to destroy cancerous tumors.
- **Carbon Dating:** Using the decay rate of Carbon-14 to determine the age of ancient organic fossils.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Medical:** Iodine-131 (goiter/thyroid), Cobalt-60 (cancer treatment).
- **Agriculture:** Phosphorus-32 (tracing nutrient uptake in plants).
- **Industry:** Checking for cracks or leaks in underground pipes using radioactive tracers.
- **Archaeology:** Estimating the age of dead plants/animals (C-14 dating).

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, you must memorize which specific isotope is used for which task. An MCQ asking "Which isotope is used to treat the thyroid gland?" is incredibly common, and you must confidently select Iodine-131 over Cobalt-60.

# Relative Atomic Mass — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This theoretical lecture introduces the concept of Relative Atomic Mass. The teacher explains how scientists weigh atoms, considering that single atoms are far too small to be placed on any physical scale.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher tells us that instead of using grams to weigh an atom (which results in absurdly small exponents like $10^{-24}$), scientists use a comparative scale. Carbon-12 is taken as the universal standard. The Relative Atomic Mass of any element is simply how heavy it is compared to $1/12th$ the mass of a Carbon-12 atom. The teacher also introduces the Atomic Mass Unit (amu).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Relative Atomic Mass:** The mass of an atom of an element compared to $1/12th$ the mass of a Carbon-12 atom.
- **Atomic Mass Unit (amu):** The standard unit of mass for atomic and molecular weights, exactly equal to $1/12th$ of a C-12 atom.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Mass of 1 Carbon-12 atom = 12 amu.
- Mass of 1 Hydrogen atom = 1.008 amu.
- Mass of 1 Oxygen atom = 15.999 amu.
- Fractional atomic masses (like Chlorine = 35.5) exist because they are the *average* mass of all the naturally occurring isotopes of that element.

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, the standard reference element is ALWAYS Carbon-12. Knowing that 1 amu = $1.66 \times 10^{-24} \text{ g}$ is also a very frequent MCQ. Furthermore, understand that relative atomic mass is an *average* of isotopes.

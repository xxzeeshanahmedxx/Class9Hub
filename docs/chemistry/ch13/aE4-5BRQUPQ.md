## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher works through the MCQs, focusing heavily on identifying the visual hazard symbols found on chemical bottles. The teacher reviews the correct sequence of actions to take during a lab emergency, such as chemical spills on the skin or a small fire, reinforcing that safety is the absolute highest priority in chemistry.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Hazard Symbol:** A universally recognized pictogram placed on chemical containers to warn of specific dangers (e.g., toxic, corrosive).
- **Protocol:** The official, safe procedure to follow during a laboratory experiment or emergency.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Never taste or sniff chemicals directly.
- Always pour Acid into Water (never Water into Acid) to prevent explosive splashing.
- If a chemical spills on the skin, wash immediately with large amounts of running water.
- Know the location of the fire extinguisher, eye wash station, and emergency exit.

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, the "Acid into Water" rule is the most universally tested lab safety question. You must remember the phrase "Always Do As You Oughta, Add Acid Into Wata." Adding water to concentrated acid causes an immediate, violent boil that shatters glass.

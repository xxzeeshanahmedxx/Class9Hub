# Introduction, Chemical Hazards in the Laboratory — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This foundational safety lecture introduces the overarching concept of Chemical Hazards in the Laboratory. The teacher explains the personal responsibility every student holds when entering a chemistry lab.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher emphasizes Personal Protective Equipment (PPE). Wearing a lab coat protects skin and clothing, while safety goggles are strictly mandatory to protect the eyes from splashes. The teacher reviews general conduct: no running, no eating or drinking in the lab, and keeping workspaces clean and uncluttered to prevent accidental spills.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Safety gear, including goggles, lab coats, and gloves, designed to protect the wearer from chemical hazards.
- **Chemical Hazard:** Any chemical agent that has the potential to cause physical or health dangers in the laboratory.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Always tie back long hair to prevent it from catching fire in a Bunsen burner.
- Read the label on a chemical bottle at least twice before using it.
- Never return unused chemicals to their original stock bottle (it causes contamination).
- Point the open end of a heated test tube away from yourself and others.

## What Students Should Remember
Well, the rule regarding "smelling" chemicals is a classic exam question. You guys must remember NEVER to stick your nose directly over a test tube. Instead, you use the "Wafting" technique—using your hand to gently fan the vapors toward your nose.

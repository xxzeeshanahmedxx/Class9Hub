# The Mole And Molar Mass — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following Avogadro's number, this highly mathematical lecture teaches students how to calculate Molar Mass. The teacher explains the formulas required to convert the mass of a substance (in grams) into the number of Moles.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir defines Molar Mass as the mass of one mole of a substance, expressed in grams per mole (g/mol). The teacher demonstrates how to calculate this by simply adding up the atomic masses of all the elements in the chemical formula. The core formula of the chapter is then introduced: $\text{Number of Moles} = \frac{\text{Given Mass}}{\text{Molar Mass}}$.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Mole (mol):** The SI unit for the amount of substance.
- **Molar Mass ($M$):** The mass of one mole of a substance (calculated by adding atomic masses and changing "amu" to "grams").
- **Given Mass ($m$):** The actual physical weight of the sample in the lab, measured in grams.

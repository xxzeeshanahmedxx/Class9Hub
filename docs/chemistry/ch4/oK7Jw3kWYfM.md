# Calculation Based on Chemical Equation — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This is the ultimate capstone lecture of Chapter 4. The teacher combines balanced chemical equations with the Mole concept to perform Stoichiometry—calculating exactly how much product will be formed from a specific amount of reactant.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher tells us that the large coefficients in a balanced equation represent the Molar Ratio. For example, $N_2 + 3H_2 \rightarrow 2NH_3$ means exactly 1 mole of Nitrogen reacts with exactly 3 moles of Hydrogen to produce exactly 2 moles of Ammonia. The teacher demonstrates the 3-step calculation method: Convert given mass to moles $\rightarrow$ Use the ratio from the balanced equation to find product moles $\rightarrow$ Convert product moles back to grams.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Stoichiometry:** The quantitative study of reactants and products in a balanced chemical reaction.
- **Molar Ratio:** The ratio of the coefficients of any two substances in a balanced chemical equation.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Step 1: Divide the given mass by its Molar Mass to get given Moles.
- Step 2: Set up a cross-multiplication ratio (e.g., If $1N_2$ gives $2NH_3$, then $0.5N_2$ gives $X$).
- Step 3: Multiply the found moles by the Molar Mass of the product to get the final answer in grams.
- The equation MUST be balanced first, or the ratio will be wrong.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: Do not skip the mole conversion! You CANNOT do mass-to-mass ratios directly (e.g., 10g of N2 does NOT give 20g of NH3 just bcuz the coefficient is 2). You must ALWAYS go through the "Mole Bridge" (Mass $\rightarrow$ Moles $\rightarrow$ Ratio $\rightarrow$ Moles $\rightarrow$ Mass).

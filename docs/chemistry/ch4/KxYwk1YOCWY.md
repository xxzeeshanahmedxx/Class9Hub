# Chemical and Empirical Formula — Class 9 Chemistry Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Chapter 4 shifts from chemical bonding to chemical mathematics. This introductory lecture covers how to mathematically represent compounds using Chemical, Molecular, and Empirical Formulas.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher tells us that a Molecular Formula shows the exact, actual number of atoms of each element in a molecule (e.g., Glucose is $C_6H_{12}O_6$). However, an Empirical Formula shows the simplest whole-number ratio of those atoms. By dividing the glucose formula by 6, the teacher derives its empirical formula: $CH_2O$. 

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Molecular Formula:** A chemical formula that shows the actual number of atoms of each element present in a molecule.
- **Empirical Formula:** The simplest whole-number ratio of atoms of each element present in a compound.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Some compounds have the exact same molecular and empirical formula (e.g., $H_2O$ cannot be simplified further).
- Ionic compounds ONLY exist as empirical formulas (e.g., $NaCl$) becuz they form massive 3D lattices, not discrete molecules.
- Benzene: Molecular = $C_6H_6$, Empirical = $CH$.
- Hydrogen Peroxide: Molecular = $H_2O_2$, Empirical = $HO$.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: You guys must remember that ionic compounds do not have molecular formulas. an examiner will trap you by asking for the molecular formula of sodium chloride. you must confidently state that because it is a 3d ionic lattice, $nacl$ is strictly an empirical formula unit.

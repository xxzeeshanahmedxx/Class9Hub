# Status of virus in Classification — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This is a highly fascinating, conceptual lecture. The teacher addresses a massive biological loophole: Viruses. Where do viruses fit in the Five Kingdom system? The answer is nowhere.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor explains that viruses are on the borderline of living and non-living. They do not have a cellular structure (no cytoplasm, no organelles). They are simply a strand of DNA or RNA wrapped in a protein coat. Outside a host cell, they are completely dead and can be crystallized like salt. However, once they inject themselves into a living cell, they "hijack" it to reproduce. Because they are not truly cells, they are NOT included in the Five-Kingdom classification.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Virus:** An infectious, non-cellular particle consisting of a nucleic acid core (DNA or RNA) surrounded by a protein coat.
- **Obligate Intracellular Parasite:** An organism (or particle) that can only reproduce *inside* a living host cell.
- **Prions/Viroids:** Even simpler infectious particles made only of protein or RNA.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Viruses are acellular (not made of cells).
- They lack the metabolic machinery to eat, grow, or reproduce on their own.
- They are excluded from all Five Kingdoms.

## What Students Should Remember
Listen carefully here: The reason viruses are excluded from the 5 kingdoms is a guaranteed board question! You must clearly state that "Viruses are acellular particles that do not possess standard cellular organization, putting them on the borderline of living and non-living things."

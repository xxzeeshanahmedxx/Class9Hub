# Domains of Living Organisims — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture acts as a rapid review, specifically focusing on the traits of the organisms inside the various Domains and Kingdoms, reinforcing the biological vocabulary required for exams.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor drills down into the specific characteristics. Monerans are singled out as the ONLY prokaryotes. The teacher reviews Fungi, emphasizing their unique absorptive nutrition (they secrete enzymes outside their body, break down dead matter, and absorb the liquid). The lack of a cell wall in Animalia is also highlighted as a defining feature.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Absorptive Heterotroph:** Organisms like fungi that secrete digestive enzymes externally and absorb the broken-down nutrients.
- **Chitin:** The tough, flexible structural carbohydrate found in the cell walls of fungi.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Fungi do NOT perform photosynthesis; they have zero chlorophyll.
- Plants store their energy as starch and have cell walls made of cellulose.
- Animals store their energy as glycogen and ingest (eat) their food directly.

## What Students Should Remember
Listen carefully here: Make sure you remember the cell wall compositions! this is a highly frequent mcq format. plant cell walls = cellulose. fungi cell walls = chitin. bacteria cell walls = peptidoglycan (murein). animal cells = no cell wall.

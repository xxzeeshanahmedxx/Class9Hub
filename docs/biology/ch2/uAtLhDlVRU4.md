# History of Classification — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture explores teh History of Classification, detailing how early scientists attempted to organize life before the invention of microscopes and modern genetics.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor traces classification back to the Greek philosopher Aristotle, who broadly classified animals based on their habitat (land, water, air). The teacher then highlights the monumental work of Carolus Linnaeus (Carl Linnaeus) in the 1700s. Linnaeus grouped organisms based on physical similarities and formally established the taxonomic hierarchy and the system of Binomial Nomenclature (scientific naming).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Aristotle:** Early philosopher who classified organisms purely on habitat.
- **Carolus Linnaeus:** The father of modern taxonomy who formalized the hierarchical system and binomial nomenclature.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Early classification was flawed (e.g., putting bats and butterflies in the same group just because they both fly).
- Linnaeus introduced the Phylum, Class, Order, Genus, and Species ranks.
- His physical grouping laid the groundwork for modern evolutionary classification.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, knowing the title "Father of Taxonomy" is a highly tested MCQ. You must associate Carolus Linnaeus with the creation of the taxonomic ranks and the scientific naming system.

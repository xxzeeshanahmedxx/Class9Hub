# Three Domain Classification System — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Before reaching the famous Five-Kingdom model, this lecture introduces a broader, more modern classification: The Three Domain System. The teacher explains how modern genetics forced scientists to add a layer even higher than a "Kingdom".

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor explains Carl Woese's 1990 system. Based on analyzing ribosomal RNA (genetics), scientists discovered that bacteria actually consist of two vastly different groups. Woese created a new, highest taxonomic rank called a "Domain." The three domains are Archaea (ancient, extreme bacteria), Bacteria (normal true bacteria), and Eukarya (everything else with a nucleus).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Domain:** The highest taxonomic rank of organisms, ranking above a Kingdom.
- **Archaea:** A domain of single-celled microorganisms that are prokaryotes but genetically distinct from true bacteria, often living in extreme environments.
- **Eukarya:** The domain comprising all organisms whose cells possess a true nucleus.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- The three domains: Archaea, Bacteria, Eukarya.
- The Eukarya domain contains four kingdoms: Protista, Fungi, Plantae, and Animalia.
- This system separates the prokaryotes into two domains because their genetic makeup is incredibly different.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, the reason for splitting the prokaryotes is a key concept. Examiners will ask why Bacteria and Archaea are in different domains despite both lacking a nucleus. You must answer that their *ribosomal RNA (genetics) and cell wall structures* are fundamentally different.

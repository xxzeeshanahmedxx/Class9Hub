# Exercise Questions Chapter 6 — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
In this final video for Chapter 6, the teacher transitions to board exam preparation. He systematically solves the theoretical Exercise Questions, including MCQs, short conceptual queries, and long descriptive questions regarding Biomolecules.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor reviews the four major classes of biomolecules (Carbs, Proteins, Lipids, Nucleic Acids), ensuring students know their monomers and functions. He focuses heavily on the structural comparison between DNA and RNA, and explains how to answer questions regarding the dietary sources of specific macromolecules.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Biochemical Synthesis:** Using the knowledge of molecular building blocks (amino acids, nucleotides, sugars) to solve textbook problems regarding cellular function and nutrition.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Reaffirming that Amino Acids are the building blocks of Proteins (linked by peptide bonds).
- Confirming that Glucose is a monosaccharide while Starch is a polysaccharide.
- Reviewing the complementary base pairing rule (A-T, C-G) for DNA questions.
- Identifying lipids as the most energy-dense biomolecules.

## What Students Should Remember
Listen carefully here: If a long question asks you to "Write a note on Nucleic Acids," you cannot just write about DNA. You MUST dedicate half of your answer to RNA, highlighting its single-stranded structure and its role in translating the DNA code into proteins.

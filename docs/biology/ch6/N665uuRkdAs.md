# Ribonucleic Acid (RNA) — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture covers the second type of nucleic acid: RNA (Ribonucleic Acid). The teacher explains how RNA differs structurally from DNA and acts as the crucial messenger boy for the cell.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor highlights three major structural differences. First, RNA is usually Single-Stranded (not a double helix). Second, its sugar is normal "Ribose," not deoxyribose. Third, and most importantly, RNA does NOT contain the base Thymine (T); instead, it uses a different base called Uracil (U). Therefore, in RNA, Adenine (A) pairs with Uracil (U). The teacher outlines three types of RNA: Messenger RNA (mRNA), Transfer RNA (tRNA), and Ribosomal RNA (rRNA).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **RNA (Ribonucleic Acid):** A single-stranded nucleic acid present in all living cells that has structural similarities to DNA but plays a direct role in protein synthesis.
- **Uracil (U):** The nitrogenous base found only in RNA, replacing the Thymine found in DNA.
- **mRNA (Messenger RNA):** The RNA that copies the code from DNA and carries it to the ribosomes.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
| Feature | DNA | RNA |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strands** | Double | Single |
| **Sugar** | Deoxyribose | Ribose |
| **Bases** | A, T, C, G | A, U, C, G |
| **Location**| Stuck in nucleus | Moves from nucleus to cytoplasm |

## What Students Should Remember
To be fair, "Differentiate between DNA and RNA" is a highly favored long question. You must draw a comparison table listing the differences in the Sugar type, the Number of Strands, and the presence of Uracil instead of Thymine.

## Final Recap & Board Exam Notes
By understanding RNA, students learn how the cell solves a massive logistical problem. Because the master DNA blueprint is too valuable to leave the nucleus, the cell uses RNA to make disposable, single-stranded copies to send out to the protein factories (ribosomes).

# Nucleic Acid — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This is a paramount lecture introducing the fourth and final class of biomolecules: Nucleic Acids. The teacher introduces the molecules that hold the "source code" of all life on Earth.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor explains that Nucleic Acids were first discovered inside the nucleus of pus cells by Friedrich Miescher. Sir defines them as massive polymers built from basic units called Nucleotides. The teacher explains that a single nucleotide has three distinct parts: a 5-carbon sugar (Ribose or Deoxyribose), a Phosphate group, and a Nitrogenous Base (Adenine, Thymine, Cytosine, Guanine, or Uracil). There are two main types of nucleic acids: DNA and RNA.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Nucleic Acid:** Complex organic molecules present in living cells, whose molecules consist of many nucleotides linked in a long chain.
- **Nucleotide:** The monomer (building block) of a nucleic acid.
- **Nitrogenous Base:** The part of the nucleotide that acts as the "letters" of the genetic alphabet (A, T, C, G, U).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- A Nucleotide = Sugar + Phosphate + Nitrogenous Base.
- They are responsible for the storage, transmission, and expression of genetic information.
- They are found in all living things and even in viruses.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: The composition of a Nucleotide is a guaranteed exam question. You MUST memorize the three specific parts: the Pentose Sugar, the Phosphate Group, and the Nitrogenous Base. If you forget one, you lose the mark!

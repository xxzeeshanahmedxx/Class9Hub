# Working of DNA and RNA — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This capstone lecture unites DNA, RNA, and Ribosomes. The teacher explains the "Central Dogma of Biology," detailing exactly how the genetic code is translated into actual, physical proteins.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir walks through the two-step process of Protein Synthesis. **Step 1 is Transcription:** Inside the nucleus, the DNA unzips slightly, and an mRNA copy of a specific gene is made. **Step 2 is Translation:** The mRNA leaves the nucleus and goes to a Ribosome in the cytoplasm. The ribosome reads the mRNA code, and tRNA molecules bring the correct Amino Acids. The ribosome links these amino acids together to form a functioning Protein.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Transcription:** The process of making an RNA copy of a gene sequence from DNA.
- **Translation:** The process of translating the sequence of an mRNA molecule to a sequence of amino acids during protein synthesis.
- **Central Dogma:** The flow of genetic information: DNA $\rightarrow$ RNA $\rightarrow$ Protein.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- DNA holds the original recipe.
- mRNA is the photocopy of the recipe sent to the kitchen.
- The Ribosome is the chef.
- Amino Acids are the raw ingredients.
- The resulting Protein is the finished cake.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, getting Transcription and Translation mixed up is a classic error. Remember: Transcription happens FIRST inside the nucleus (writing the script). Translation happens SECOND at the ribosome (translating the script into a physical protein).

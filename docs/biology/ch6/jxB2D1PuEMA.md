# Biomolecules — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Chapter 6 introduces Biochemistry. This introductory lecture zooms back down to the molecular level, explaining Biomolecules—the specific, massive chemical compounds that build and fuel every living cell on Earth.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor explains that while the universe has over 100 elements, life is primarily built from just six: Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen, Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Sulfur (CHONPS). Sir defines Biomolecules as the organic macromolecules synthesized by living things. The lecture outlines the four major categories: Carbohydrates, Proteins, Lipids (fats), and Nucleic Acids (DNA/RNA).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Biomolecules:** Organic molecules produced by living organisms that are essential for life.
- **Macromolecule:** A very large molecule, such as a protein or nucleic acid, built from smaller chemical building blocks.
- **Organic Compound:** Any chemical compound containing carbon-hydrogen bonds.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Water is the most abundant *inorganic* molecule in living things (60-70% of a cell).
- Proteins make up the physical structure of the cell.
- Carbohydrates provide instant energy.
- Nucleic acids store the genetic code.

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, Make sure you remember the "chonps" elements. a frequent mcq asks which elements constitute the vast majority of biological mass. you must identify carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, and nitrogen as the absolute primary building blocks of life.

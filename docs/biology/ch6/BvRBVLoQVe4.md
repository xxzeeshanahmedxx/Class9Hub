# Sources and Functions of Carbohydrates — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Moving from chemical structure to diet, this lecture details the natural Sources and vital Functions of Carbohydrates in the human body and the ecosystem.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor explains that plants are the ultimate source of all carbohydrates, synthesizing glucose via photosynthesis using sunlight. The teacher lists common dietary sources: wheat, rice, potatoes (high in starch), and fruits (high in fructose/sucrose). Sir then emphasizes their functions: they act as the primary, immediate energy source (1 gram provides ~4 kilocalories) and form the structural framework of plants (cellulose) and insects (chitin).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Photosynthesis:** The biological process by which plants use sunlight to synthesize foods (carbohydrates) from carbon dioxide and water.
- **Dietary Fiber (Roughage):** Indigestible plant carbohydrates (cellulose) that aid in human digestion and bowel health.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Energy Source:** Glucose is instantly burned in mitochondria to make ATP.
- **Storage:** Plants store extra carbs as Starch; Humans/Animals store them in the liver as Glycogen.
- **Structure:** Cellulose builds plant cell walls.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, the difference in storage molecules is highly tested. If asked how animals store excess carbohydrates, do NOT say starch. Plants store Starch; Animals store **Glycogen**. This distinction is critical for full marks.

# Deoxyribonucleic Acid (DNA) — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the introduction to nucleic acids, this lecture focuses exclusively on the most famous molecule in biology: DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid). The teacher details its structure and how it stores the genetic code.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir introduces the Watson and Crick Double Helix Model. DNA is composed of two long strands of nucleotides twisted around each other like a spiral staircase. The "handrails" of the stairs are made of alternating sugar and phosphate groups, while the "steps" are formed by Nitrogenous Bases linking the two strands together. The teacher emphasizes the strict Base Pairing Rule: Adenine (A) always pairs with Thymine (T) via two hydrogen bonds, and Cytosine (C) always pairs with Guanine (G) via three hydrogen bonds.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid):** The molecule carrying genetic instructions for the development and functioning of all living organisms.
- **Double Helix:** The twisted-ladder shape of the DNA molecule.
- **Complementary Base Pairing:** The strict rule that A bonds with T, and C bonds with G.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- DNA contains the sugar "Deoxyribose" (meaning it has one less oxygen than normal ribose).
- It is found almost exclusively inside the nucleus (with small amounts in mitochondria/chloroplasts).
- The sequence of these A, T, C, and G bases is the actual "code" that tells the cell how to build proteins.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, the Base Pairing Rule is absolutely vital. If an exam gives you one strand of DNA that reads "A-A-C-T-G", you must be able to write the complementary strand by applying the rule: "T-T-G-A-C". Remembering that A goes with T, and C goes with G is mandatory.

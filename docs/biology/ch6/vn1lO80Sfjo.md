# Carbohydrates — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture focuses on the first major class of biomolecules: Carbohydrates. The teacher explains the chemical structure of these "hydrated carbons" and why they are the primary energy source for all life.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor breaks down the name: "Carbo" (Carbon) and "Hydrate" (Water, $H_2O$). The general formula is $C_x(H_2O)_y$. Sir categorizes them into three groups based on size. Monosaccharides are simple, single sugars (like Glucose). Oligosaccharides are composed of 2 to 10 simple sugars linked together (like Sucrose/table salt). Polysaccharides are massive, complex chains of hundreds of sugars (like Starch and Cellulose).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Carbohydrates:** Organic compounds occurring in foods and living tissues, consisting of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen.
- **Monosaccharide:** The simplest form of carbohydrate, cannot be hydrolyzed further (e.g., Glucose).
- **Polysaccharide:** A complex carbohydrate composed of long chains of monosaccharide units (e.g., Starch, Glycogen).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Glucose ($C_6H_{12}O_6$) is the most importnt monosaccharide; it is the direct fuel for cellular respiration.
- Sucrose (table sugar) is a disaccharide (an oligosaccharide made of 2 rings).
- Cellulose is a polysaccharide that makes up plant cell walls; humans cannot digest it, so it acts as dietary fiber.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, categorizing glucose vs. starch is a guaranteed board question. You must explicitly state that Glucose is a simple Monosaccharide (instant energy), while Starch is a massive Polysaccharide used by plants to *store* energy long-term.

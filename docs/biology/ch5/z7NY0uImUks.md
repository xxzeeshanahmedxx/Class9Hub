# Emergent Properties — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the hierarchy of life, this lecture introduces a profound scientific concept: Emergent Properties. The teacher explains why studying a whole system is very different from studying its individual, disassembled parts.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir defines an emergent property as a characteristic that a complex system has, but which the individual parts do not have. The classic example is a bicycle: a pile of gears, a chain, and two wheels cannot transport you anywhere. But when put together correctly, the "property of transportation" emerges. In biology, individual heart cells cannot pump blood on their own, but when organized into a heart, the emergent property of "pumping" appears.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Emergent Property:** A property that a complex system or collection has, but which the individual members do not possess.
- **Synergy:** The concept that the whole is greater than the sum of its parts.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Amino acids alone cannot digest food, but when folded into a specific Enzyme (protein), they gain catalytic properties.
- Consciousness and thought are emergent properties of billions of interconnected brain cells.
- This is why organisms cannot be studied purely by looking at isolated cells in a petri dish.

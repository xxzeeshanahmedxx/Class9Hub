# Ribosome — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This short but crucial lecture focuses on Ribosomes. The teacher explains the function of the cell's tiny "protein factories," detailing how they read genetic instructions to build the physical components of life.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir describes ribosomes as tiny, granular structures found in massive numbers in all cells (both prokaryotes and eukaryotes). They do NOT have a membrane around them. They are composed of ribosomal RNA (rRNA) and proteins. The teacher explains their single, vital function: Protein Synthesis. They take the mRNA copies from the nucleus and translate them into physical protein chains.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Ribosome:** A tiny, non-membrane-bound particle consisting of RNA and associated proteins found in large numbers in the cytoplasm.
- **Protein Synthesis:** The process by which biological cells generate new proteins.
- **Nucleolus:** The region inside the nucleus where ribosomes are manufactured.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Ribosomes can float freely in the cytoplasm or be attached to the Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum (RER).
- Free ribosomes make proteins for use *inside* the cell.
- Attached ribosomes make proteins meant to be exported *outside* the cell.
- Eukaryotic ribosomes are slightly larger (80S) than prokaryotic ribosomes (70S).

## What Students Should Remember
Listen carefully here: The fact that ribosomes have NO membrane is incredibly important. This is exactly why they are found in both Eukaryotes (which have membrane-bound organelles) AND Prokaryotes (bacteria, which don't). It is a favorite MCQ trap!

# Division of Labour within and across Cells — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following cell specialization, this lecture zooms out to the macro level. The teacher introduces the Division of Labour, explaining the biological hierarchy from cells, to tissues, to organs, to organ systems.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor explains that specialized cells do not work alone; they group together. A group of similar cells working together forms a Tissue (like muscle tissue). Different tissues working together form an Organ (like the stomach, which has muscle, nerve, and glandular tissues). Different organs working together form an Organ System (like the digestive system: stomach, intestines, liver). Finally, all systems combine to form the whole Organism.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Tissue:** A group of similar specialized cells performing a common function.
- **Organ:** A structure composed of different types of tissues that work together to perform a specific, complex function.
- **Organ System:** A group of organs that work together to perform a major body function (e.g., Circulatory System).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Sequence:** Cells $\rightarrow$ Tissues $\rightarrow$ Organs $\rightarrow$ Organ Systems $\rightarrow$ Organism.
- Division of labour ensures efficiency. The heart doesn't have to worry about digesting food, and the stomach doesn't have to worry about pumping blood.
- Single-celled organisms (like Amoeba) have Division of Labour *within* the cell (organelles doing different jobs), but no tissues.

## What Students Should Remember
Listen carefully here: You must be able to list the hierarchy in the exact correct order. A very common MCQ asks for the correct sequence of biological organization. Always pick: Cell $\rightarrow$ Tissue $\rightarrow$ Organ $\rightarrow$ Organ System.

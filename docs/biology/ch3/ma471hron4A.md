# Cell Membrane — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Moving inward, this lecture covers the Cell Membrane (Plasma Membrane). The teacher explains this incredibly complex, semi-permeable border that exists in absolutely every living cell on Earth.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor introduces the "Fluid Mosaic Model." The membrane is not a solid wall; it is a fluid, flexible double-layer of lipids (fats) with protein molecules floating inside it like icebergs in a sea. Sir explains its primary function: it acts as a selective gatekeeper, deciding exactly which molecules are allowed to enter or exit the cell (Selective Permeability).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Cell Membrane:** The semipermeable membrane enclosing the cytoplasm of a cell.
- **Fluid Mosaic Model:** The accepted scientific model describing the structure of the cell membrane as a mosaic of components (phospholipids, cholesterol, proteins).
- **Semi-Permeable (Selectively Permeable):** Allowing certain substances to pass through while blocking others.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- The membrane is primarily composed of a Phospholipid Bilayer.
- Proteins embedded in the membrane act as channels or pumps to move large molecules (like glucose) inside.
- Small uncharged molecules (like oxygen and carbon dioxide) can slip directly through the lipid layer.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, the term "Fluid Mosaic Model" is essential. When asked to describe the cell membrane, you must use this specific term and explain that "fluid" refers to the flexible lipid layer, and "mosaic" refers to the scattered protein molecules embedded within it.

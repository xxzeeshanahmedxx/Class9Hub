# Plastids — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Shifting focus to Botany, this lecture explores Plastids. The teacher explains these massive, colorful organelles that exist ONLY in plant cells and some protists, covering how plants capture sunlight and store food.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor divides plastids into three types. **Chloroplasts** contain the green pigment chlorophyll and are the sites of photosynthesis. **Chromoplasts** contain bright pigments (red, orange, yellow) and give color to flower petals and fruits to attract pollinators. **Leucoplasts** are colorless plastids found in underground roots and stems, used strictly to store food (starch, proteins, and lipids).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Plastid:** A class of double-membrane organelles found in the cells of plants and algae.
- **Chloroplast:** The specific plastid responsible for photosynthesis.
- **Chlorophyll:** The green pigment inside chloroplasts that traps solar energy.
- **Chromoplast:** A colored plastid responsible for pigment synthesis and storage.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Chloroplasts, like mitochondria, have their own DNA and ribosomes.
- Inside the chloroplast are stacks of disks called Thylakoids (the stack is a Granum), surrounded by fluid called Stroma.
- Leucoplasts are heavily abundant in potatoes and radishes.

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, distinguishing between the three types of plastids is heavily tested in matching questions. Remember: Chloro = Green (Leaves/Food making), Chromo = Color (Flowers/Attraction), Leuco = White/Colorless (Roots/Storage).

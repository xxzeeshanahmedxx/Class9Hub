## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor creates a T-chart contrasting the two kingdoms. Plant cells have a rigid cell wall, a large central vacuole, and plastids (chloroplasts) for making food. Because of the massive central vacuole, their nucleus is pushed to the periphery (side). Animal cells lack a cell wall and plastids, have many small vacuoles, contain centrioles for division, and keep their nucleus dead center.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Flagella:** Long, whip-like tails used by some cells (like sperm or bacteria) for swimming.
- **Cilia:** Short, hair-like projections that beat together to move the cell or sweep fluid past it (like in human lungs).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
| Feature | Plant Cell | Animal Cell |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cell Wall** | Present (Cellulose) | Absent |
| **Plastids** | Present | Absent |
| **Vacuole** | One large central vacuole | Many small vacuoles |
| **Centrioles** | Absent | Present |
| **Nucleus Position** | Peripheral (pushed to side) | Central |

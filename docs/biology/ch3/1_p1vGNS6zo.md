# Cytoplasm — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Once inside the membrane, this lecture explores the Cytoplasm. The teacher describes the physical environment where all the cellular organelles live and where most chemical reactions take place.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor defines the cytoplasm as the jelly-like, semi-transparent substance that fills the inside of the cell. It consists mainly of water (about 80%) along with dissolved salts, proteins, and sugars. The teacher distinguishes between the liquid portion (Cytosol) and the solid structures floating in it (Organelles). 

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Cytoplasm:** The thick, jelly-like fluid enclosed by the cell membrane, excluding the nucleus.
- **Cytosol:** The aqueous (water-based) component of the cytoplasm.
- **Metabolism:** The chemical processes occurring within a living cell, many of which happen in the cytoplasm.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- It acts as a storehouse for vital chemicals and nutrients.
- It provides a physical medium for organelles to float and move around (Cyclosis/Cytoplasmic streaming).
- Glycolysis (the first step of cellular respiration) occurs entirely within the cytoplasm.

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, if an exam asks for the functions of the cytoplasm, you must list two things: First, it acts as a site for biochemical reactions (like glycolysis). Second, it holds and protects the cell organelles.

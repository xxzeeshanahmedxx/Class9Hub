# Science as a Collaborative Field — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture focuses on the history and methodology of science. The teacher explores Science as a Collaborative Field, highlighting the contributions of famous Muslim scientists to early biology and medicine.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor details the biographies and major works of three prominent scientists: Jabir Bin Hayan, Abdul Malik Asmai, and Ali bin Isa. Sir explains how their early collaborative work in botany and zoology laid the foundation for modern science. The teacher emphasizes that modern science relies on global cooperation, publishing research so others can build upon it.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Collaboration:** Working together to create or discover something.
- **Jabir Bin Hayan:** Known for his early work in chemistry and writing books on plants and animals.
- **Abdul Malik Asmai:** Considered the first Muslim scientist to study animals in detail.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Jabir Bin Hayan:** Wrote *Al-Nabatat* (plants) and *Al-Haywan* (animals).
- **Abdul Malik Asmai:** Wrote *Al-Ibil* (camel), *Al-Khail* (horse), and *Al-Wahoosh* (animal).
- Science is cumulative; new discoveries are always built upon the work of previous generations.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, matching the scientist to their famous book is a guaranteed MCQ. You must memorize that Abdul Malik Asmai wrote *Al-Wahoosh* and Jabir Bin Hayan wrote *Al-Nabatat*.

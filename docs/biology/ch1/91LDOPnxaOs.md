# Scientific Method — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This is a critical, foundational lecture. The teacher introduces the Biological Method (Scientific Method), the strict, logical sequence of steps that biologists use to solve problems and discover new facts.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor outlines the steps: Recognizing the biological problem, making Observations (qualitative and quantitative), forming a Hypothesis (an educated guess), making a Deduction ("if... then" logic), conducting an Experiment, and finally, analyzing the Data to reach a Conclusion. The teacher heavily emphasizes that Experiments are the only way to prove a hypothesis correct or incorrect.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Biological Method:** The step-by-step scientific method used to resolve biological problems.
- **Observation:** Gathering data using the 5 senses.
- **Hypothesis:** A tentative explanation of observations that can be tested.
- **Deduction:** The logical consequences drawn from a hypothesis.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Quantitative observations (numbers/measurements) are better than Qualitative ones because they are exact and unchanging.
- A hypothesis MUST be testable and falsifiable.
- An experiment always includes a "Control Group" and an "Experimental Group" to compare results.

## What Students Should Remember
Listen carefully here: You guys must memorize the exact order of the steps. Furthermore, examiners love asking why quantitative observations are superior. You must answer: "Because they are measurable, invariable, and can be recorded in numbers."

# Relation of Biology with Other Science — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture explores teh Interdisciplinary Sciences. The teacher explains that modern biology cannot exist in isolation; it heavily relies on physics, chemistry, and mathematics to fully explain living systems.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor introduces several interdisciplinary fields. Biophysics applies the laws of physics to biology (e.g., how the limbs act as levers). Biochemistry studies the chemical reactions inside living things (e.g., respiration or photosynthesis). Biomathematics uses math formulas to analyze biological data, and Biogeography studies where specific organisms live on Earth.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Interdisciplinary Science:** A field that combines two or more academic disciplines.
- **Biophysics:** The application of the laws of physics to biological phenomena.
- **Biochemistry:** The study of chemical processes within and relating to living organisms.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Bioeconomics:** Calculating the cost vs. profit of biological projects (like crop yields).
- **Biomathematics (Biometry):** Using statistics to analyze DNA or population growth.
- The laws of thermodynamics (physics) dictate how energy flows through a food chain.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, examiners love asking for examples. If asked about Biophysics, you must explicitly mention "the similarity between the working principles of the lever in physics and human limbs in biology."

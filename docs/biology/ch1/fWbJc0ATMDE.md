# Theory and Law (Principle) — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the scientific method, this lecture explains what happens when a hypothesis survives repeated testing. The teacher differentiates between a Hypothesis, a Theory, and a Scientific Law.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir explains that if a hypothesis is tested repeatedly over many years and is never rejected, it becomes a Theory (like the Theory of Evolution). A theory is highly supported but can still theoretically be disproven. However, if a theory survives all skepticism and is universally accepted as an undeniable fact of nature, it becomes a Scientific Law or Principle (like Mendel's Laws of Inheritance).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Theory:** A hypothesis that has been tested repeatedly and supported by a massive amount of evidence.
- **Scientific Law (Principle):** A uniform or constant fact of nature; an irrefutable theory.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- A hypothesis is an initial educated guess.
- A theory is a well-supported explanation (e.g., Cell Theory).
- A Law is an undeniable, universal fact (e.g., Hardy-Weinberg Law).
- Biology has very few "Laws" compared to Physics because living organisms are highly unpredictable.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: The reason why Biology has fewer laws than Physics/Math is a very common conceptual question. You must explain that biological systems are incredibly complex and constantly evolving, making it difficult to establish absolute, invariable universal laws.

# Cell Cycle — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Chapter 4 introduces the mechanics of cellular reproduction. This introductory lecture covers the Cell Cycle, explaining the entire lifespan of a cell from the moment it is "born" until the moment it divides to create new cells.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor divides the cell cycle into two massive phases: Interphase (the growing and resting phase) and the M-Phase (the actual splitting/dividing phase). Sir emphasizes that cells spend 90% of their life in Interphase. Interphase is further broken down into three sub-phases: G1 (cell grows), S-phase (Synthesis, where the DNA is perfectly copied), and G2 (final preparations for division).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Cell Cycle:** The series of events that take place in a cell leading to its division and duplication.
- **Interphase:** The longest phase of the cell cycle where the cell grows and copies its DNA.
- **S-Phase (Synthesis Phase):** The critical phase of Interphase where DNA replication occurs.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **G1 (Gap 1):** Cell grows, makes more proteins and organelles.
- **S Phase:** Chromosomes duplicate (from single threads to X-shapes).
- **G2 (Gap 2):** Cell checks for DNA errors and prepares spindle proteins.
- **G0 Phase:** A resting state where some cells (like adult nerve cells) exit the cycle and never divide again.

## What Students Should Remember
Well, the S-Phase is the most important part of Interphase for exams. If asked "When does DNA replication occur?", you must specify the S-Phase. If DNA is not copied perfectly here, the resulting daughter cells will be mutated.

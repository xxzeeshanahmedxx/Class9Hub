# Significance Of Mitosis — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This conceptual lecture focuses on the "Why." Having learned the mechanics of Mitosis, the teacher explains the Significance of Mitosis—why this specific type of cloning division is absolutely vital for life on Earth.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir explains that because Mitosis ensures the chromosome number remains perfectly constant, it is used for three primary purposes: Development and Growth (a baby growing into an adult), Cell Replacement (skin cells replacing dead ones), and Regeneration (a starfish growing back a lost arm). The teacher also highlights that single-celled organisms and some plants use mitosis for Asexual Reproduction.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Significance:** The biological importance or real-world application of a cellular process.
- **Regeneration:** The process of renewal, restoration, and growth that makes genomes, cells, and organs resilient to natural fluctuations or events that cause damage.
- **Asexual Reproduction:** Reproduction involving only one parent, producing offspring that are genetically identical to the parent via mitosis.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Mitosis keeps the genetic code 100% identical.
- Without mitosis, wounds would never heal, and broken bones would never knit back together.
- In vegetative propagation (cutting a branch to grow a new tree), the plant is using mitosis.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, when asked for the significance of mitosis in a long question, you must use the major headings: "Growth, Healing of Wounds, Replacement of Cells, and Asexual Reproduction." Providing a brief one-sentence explanation under each heading guarantees full marks.

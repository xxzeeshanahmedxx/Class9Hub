# Errors in Mitosis — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This medical-focused lecture explores the dark side of cell division: Errors in Mitosis. The teacher explains what happens when the strict regulations of the Cell Cycle break down, leading to the formation of tumors and cancer.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor explains that cells have genetic "checkpoints." If a cell mutates and ignores these stop signals, it begins dividing rapidly and uncontrollably, forming a mass of abnormal cells called a Tumor. The teacher distinguishes between Benign Tumors (wich stay in one place and are generally harmless) and Malignant Tumors (Cancer), which aggressively invade surrounding tissues and spread to other parts of the body.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Tumor:** An abnormal mass of tissue resulting from uncontrolled, unchecked cell division.
- **Benign Tumor:** A non-cancerous tumor that remains localized in its original location.
- **Malignant Tumor:** A cancerous tumor consisting of cells that invade neighboring tissues.
- **Metastasis:** The dangerous process where cancer cells break away from a malignant tumor, travel through the bloodstream, and start new tumors elsewhere in the body.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- All cancer is fundamentally a disease of broken mitosis.
- Mutations in the DNA (caused by radiation, chemicals, or viruses) are what break the cellular checkpoints.
- Metastasis is what makes cancer so difficult to treat, as it is no longer isolated to one organ.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, the definition of Metastasis is incredibly important for exams. It is the defining feature of a malignant (cancerous) tumor. You must describe it as the "spreading of disease from one organ to another."

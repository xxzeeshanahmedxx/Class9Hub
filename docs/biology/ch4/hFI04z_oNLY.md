# Comparison between Mitosis & Meiosis — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This is a high-yield exam preparation lecture. The teacher creates a direct, side-by-side Comparison between Mitosis and Meiosis, cementing the differences between the two forms of cellular division.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor sets up a comparative table. Mitosis occurs in somatic (body) cells, involves one division, produces 2 diploid ($2n$) cells, has no crossing over, and creates identical clones. Meiosis occurs in germ-line (sex) cells, involves two divisions, produces 4 haploid ($1n$) cells, features crossing over in Prophase I, and creates genetically unique gametes.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Somatic Cells:** Any cell of a living organism other than the reproductive cells (divides by Mitosis).
- **Germ-line Cells:** The cells in the reproductive organs that develop into eggs and sperm (divides by Meiosis).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
| Feature | Mitosis | Meiosis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cell Type** | Somatic (Body) Cells | Germ-line (Sex) Cells |
| **Number of Divisions**| 1 | 2 |
| **Daughter Cells** | 2 | 4 |
| **Genetic Makeup** | Identical to parent | Genetically unique |
| **Chromosome #** | Remains the same ($2n$) | Reduced to half ($1n$) |
| **Crossing Over** | No | Yes (in Prophase I) |

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, drawing a T-chart like the one above is the absolute best way to answer this question in a board exam. Writing long paragraphs usually results in missed points. Ensure you explicitly contrast the chromosome numbers ($2n$ vs $1n$).

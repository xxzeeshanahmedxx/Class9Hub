# Meiosis — Class 9 Biology Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture introduces the second type of cell division: Meiosis. The teacher explains this highly specialized division that is used exclusively for sexual reproduction to create sperm and egg cells.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir explains that if a human sperm (46 chromosomes) merged with an egg (46), the baby would have 92 chromosomes and die. To fix this, Meiosis cuts the chromosome number exactly in half (from Diploid 2n to Haploid 1n). The instructor details that Meiosis has two full rounds of division (Meiosis I and Meiosis II). Crucially, during Prophase I, "Crossing Over" occurs, where paired chromosomes physically swap pieces of DNA, ensuring every sperm and egg is genetically unique.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Meiosis:** A type of cell division that results in four daughter cells each with half the number of chromosomes of the parent cell.
- **Diploid (2n):** A cell containing two complete sets of chromosomes (e.g., normal body cells).
- **Haploid (1n):** A cell containing only one set of chromosomes (e.g., gametes/sex cells).
- **Crossing Over:** The exchange of genetic material between homologous chromosomes during Prophase I.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Meiosis produces 4 daughter cells (Mitosis only produces 2).
- The daughter cells are genetically DIFFERENT from the parent cell and from each other.
- Meiosis I separates the homologous chromosome pairs; Meiosis II separates the chromatids (like mitosis).

## What Students Should Remember
Listen carefully here: Crossing Over is the absolute most important event in Meiosis. If an exam asks why you don't look exactly like your siblings, you must explain that "Crossing over during Prophase I of Meiosis creates new, unique genetic combinations in every gamete."

# Basic hardware related issues — Class 9 Computer Science Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Getting into specific problems, this lecture covers Basic Hardware Related Issues. The teacher explains how to identify physical malfunctions inside teh computer casing.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir discusses the most common hardware faults. If the computer turns on but the screen is black, it might be a loose VGA/HDMI cable or faulty RAM. If the computer randomly restarts, it is often due to overheating (clogged CPU fan) or a failing Power Supply Unit (PSU). The teacher explains how to interpret beep codes during startup.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **POST (Power-On Self-Test):** A diagnostic testing sequence run by a computer's BIOS to check hardware before booting the OS.
- **Beep Codes:** Audio signals given by the motherboard speaker to indicate specific hardware failures.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Continuous beeping usually points to a RAM (memory) error.
- A computer freezing or shutting down under heavy load is usually an overheating/fan issue.
- "No Boot Device Found" means the hard drive is disconnected or broken.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, identifying beep codes and overheating symptoms is crucial for MCQs. If the question mentions "random restarts after 10 minutes of gaming," the answer is almost always "CPU Overheating due to a faulty fan or dust."

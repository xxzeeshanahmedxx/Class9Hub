# Hardware diagnosis and maintenance — Class 9 Computer Science Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following problem identification, this lecture teaches the solutions: Hardware Diagnosis and Maintenance. The teacher explains the physical procedures required to keep a computer healthy and fix the issues discussed previously.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor demonstrates the importance of cleaning dust from the motherboard and fans using compressed air, as dust traps heat. The teacher explains how to safely reseat RAM modules by taking them out, cleaning the gold contacts with an eraser, and snapping them back in. Sir also emphasizes anti-static safety—touching a metal case to ground yourself before touching sensitive computer chips.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Reseating:** The process of removing a hardware component and plugging it back in firmly to fix loose connections.
- **Electrostatic Discharge (ESD):** A sudden flow of electricity between two objects, which can permanently destroy sensitive computer chips.
- **Thermal Paste:** A compound applied between the CPU and the heat sink to ensure proper heat transfer.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Always unplug the computer and hold the power button for 5 seconds to drain residual power before opening the case.
- Never use a wet cloth to clean internal circuits; use compressed air or isopropyl alcohol.
- Replace the thermal paste every few years if the CPU is overheating.

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) is a major exam topic. You must remember that humans carry static electricity that can instantly kill a motherboard. Always mention "using an anti-static wrist strap" or "grounding yourself" before performing maintenance.

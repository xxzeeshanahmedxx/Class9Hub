# Introduction to data science — Class 9 Computer Science Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This theoretical lecture formally defines the field of Data Science. The teacher explains how statistics, computer science, and business knowledge merge together to predict the future based on past data.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir defines Data Science as the extraction of actionable knowledge directly from data. The teacher explains the lifecycle: gathering raw data, cleaning it, analyzing it, and using machine learning algorithms to make predictions. Examples include Netflix predicting what movie you want to watch next, or hospitals predicting disease outbreaks based on search engine trends.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Data Science:** An interdisciplinary field that uses scientific methods, algorithms, and systems to extract knowledge and insights from structural and unstructured data.
- **Actionable Insight:** Information extracted from data that can be acted upon to improve a business or system.
- **Big Data:** Extremely large data sets that cannot be analyzed using traditional software.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Data Science requires programming skills (like Python/R), statistical math, and domain expertise.
- It converts raw "Data" into "Information", and finally into "Knowledge".
- Every time you click, swipe, or buy something online, you are generating data for data scientists to analyze.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, distinguishing between Data and Information is critical for short answers. Data is the raw, meaningless numbers (e.g., 45, 90, 80). Information is data processed with context (e.g., Ali's test scores are 45, 90, and 80).

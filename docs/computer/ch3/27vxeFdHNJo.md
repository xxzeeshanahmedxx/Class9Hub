# Networking Devices — Class 9 Computer Science Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This hardware-focused lecture covers Networking Devices. The teacher explains the physical equipment required to connect multiple computers together and direct traffic on the network.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor introduces devices that act as the "traffic cops" of the internet. A Hub broadcasts data to every connected computer, while a Switch is smarter and sends data *only* to the specific computer that needs it. The teacher then introduces the Router, which connects completely different networks together (like connecting your home network to the global Internet).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Switch:** A networking device that connects devices on a single computer network and uses packet switching to forward data to the correct destination.
- **Router:** A networking device that forwards data packets between entirely different computer networks.
- **Modem:** A device that modulates and demodulates analog carrier signals to encode and decode digital information.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Hub:** Dumb, slow, causes network congestion (broadcasts to all).
- **Switch:** Smart, fast, directs traffic efficiently (sends only to the target).
- **Router:** Connects networks together (the gateway to the internet).
- Network Interface Cards (NICs) must be installed inside every computer to connect to a network.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, the difference between a Switch and a Router is a guaranteed board question. You guys must remember: a Switch connects computers *within* the same building/network. A Router connects *different* networks together (like your house to the ISP).

# Types of networks — Class 9 Computer Science Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture categorizes networks based on their geographical size. The teacher explains the physical differences between a small home network and the massive global internet.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The instructor breaks networks down into three primary types: LAN, MAN, and WAN. A LAN (Local Area Network) covers a small area like a house or a school building. A MAN (Metropolitan Area Network) covers an entire city, often used by cable TV providers. A WAN (Wide Area Network) covers countries or continents, connecting multiple LANs together. The Internet itself is the ultimate WAN.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **LAN (Local Area Network):** A network connecting computers within a limited geographical area.
- **MAN (Metropolitan Area Network):** A network spanning a physical area larger than a LAN but smaller than a WAN, such as a city.
- **WAN (Wide Area Network):** A network that extends over a large geographical distance.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **LAN:** Fast speeds, high security, cheap to set up (uses Ethernet/Wi-Fi).
- **MAN:** Medium speeds, connects multiple buildings across a city.
- **WAN:** Slower speeds due to distance, very expensive, uses satellites and undersea cables.
- **PAN (Personal Area Network):** A very small network (like connecting a phone to a Bluetooth speaker).

## What Students Should Remember
To be fair, examiners love asking for real-world examples to differentiate these networks. Remember: A school computer lab is a LAN. A city's cable TV network is a MAN. The global Internet is a WAN.

# Transmission Modes — Class 9 Computer Science Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture explains Transmission Modes—the specific directional rules governing how data flows between devices on a network. 

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir introduces three distinct modes. In Simplex mode, data flows in only one direction (like a radio or TV broadcast). In Half-Duplex mode, data flows in both directions, but NOT at the same time (like a walkie-talkie). In Full-Duplex mode, data flows in both directions simultaneously (like a modern telephone call).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Simplex Mode:** Unidirectional data transmission (Sender to Receiver only).
- **Half-Duplex Mode:** Bidirectional data transmission, but only one direction at a time.
- **Full-Duplex Mode:** Bidirectional, simultaneous data transmission.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Simplex Example:** Keyboard sending data to the monitor; television broadcasting.
- **Half-Duplex Example:** Police walkie-talkies (you must say "over" and wait to receive).
- **Full-Duplex Example:** Cell phone calls; internet browsing (downloading and uploading at the same time).

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, the Walkie-Talkie example is the absolute best way to explain Half-Duplex in an exam. It perfectly illustrates the "both ways, but not simultaneously" concept, wich examiners always look for in short-answer questions.

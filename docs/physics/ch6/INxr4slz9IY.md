# Hooke's Law — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Building on stress and strain, this lecture introduces Hooke's Law. The teacher explores the mathematical relationship between the force applied to a solid (specifically a spring or wire) and how much it stretches.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir states Hooke’s Law: Within the elastic limit, the Strain produced in a body is directly proportional to the applied Stress. The teacher explains the formula and introduces the constant of proportionality, known as Young’s Modulus. The "Elastic Limit" is heavily emphasized as the point of no return for a material.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Hooke's Law:** Stress $\propto$ Strain (within the elastic limit).
- **Elastic Limit:** The maximum stress a material can withstand without undergoing permanent deformation.
- **Young's Modulus ($Y$):** The ratio of tensile stress to tensile strain ($Y = \frac{Stress}{Strain}$).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- If you stretch a spring too far (past the elastic limit), Hooke's Law fails, and the spring stays permanently stretched.
- Formula for Young's Modulus: $Y = \frac{F \times L}{A \times \Delta L}$.
- SI unit of Young's Modulus is N/m² (the same as Stress, cuz Strain has no unit).

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, The phrase "within the elastic limit" must always be included when writing the definition of Hooke's Law in an exam. Without that phrase, the law is scientifically incorrect.

# Deformation of Solids — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This introductory lecture for Chapter 6 explores how solid matter reacts when subjected to external forces. Moving away from moving blocks, the teacher now focuses on the internal structure of materials and how they bend, stretch, or break.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher defines Deformation as any change in the size or shape of an object due to an applied force. The teacher then introduces two new concepts that cause and describe this deformation: Stress and Strain. Stress is the force applied per unit area, while Strain is the resulting fractional change in the object's dimensions.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Deformation:** The change in shape or size of an object due to an applied force.
- **Stress:** The restoring force per unit area set up inside a body ($Stress = \frac{F}{A}$).
- **Strain:** The ratio of the change in dimension to the original dimension (e.g., Tensile Strain = $\frac{\Delta L}{L}$).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- SI unit of Stress is N/m² or Pascal (Pa).
- Strain has NO units because it is a ratio of two identical lengths (m/m).
- Elastic deformation is temporary; plastic deformation is permanent.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, The most common mistake is giving a unit to Strain. Students really need to explicitly remember and write down that Strain is a dimensionless, unitless ratio.

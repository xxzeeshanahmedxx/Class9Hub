# Density — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lesson shifts focus to the mass distribution within materials by introducing Density. The teacher explains why a small block of iron feels heavier than a massive block of wood.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir defines Density as the mass of a substance per unit volume. It is a measure of how tightly packed the matter is within an object. The teacher derives the formula ($d = \frac{m}{V}$) and explains that density is a unique, identifying property of a pure substance, regardless of its size.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Density ($d$ or $\rho$):** The mass per unit volume of a substance.
- **Volume ($V$):** The amount of 3D space an object occupies.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Formula: Density = Mass / Volume.
- SI Unit of density is kg/m³.
- Density of pure water is $1000 \text{ kg/m}^3$ (or $1 \text{ g/cm}^3$).
- Objects with a density lower than water will float; those with higher density will sink.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, Unit conversion is critical here. Converting g/cm³ to the standard SI unit of kg/m³ requires multiplying by 1000. Students really need to ensure their units match before comparing densities in numerical problems.

# Pressure — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following teh concept of density, this lecture introduces Pressure. The teacher explains the difference between total force and how that force is distributed over a surface area.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher uses practical examples: a sharp knife cuts easily, while a blunt knife requires immense force. The difference is the surface area. Pressure is defined as the force acting perpendicularly on a unit area of a surface. The teacher derives the formula $P = \frac{F}{A}$.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Pressure ($P$):** The perpendicular force acting on a unit surface area.
- **Pascal (Pa):** The SI unit of pressure ($1 \text{ Pa} = 1 \text{ N/m}^2$).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Pressure is directly proportional to Force (more push = more pressure).
- Pressure is inversely proportional to Area (smaller area = higher pressure).
- This is why drawing pins have sharp points (tiny area = massive pressure) and tanks have wide tracks (huge area = low pressure to avoid sinking in mud).

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, Pressure is a scalar quantity. Even though it is derived from a vector (Force), pressure acts in all directions within fluids, so it does not have a single directional vector.

# Measurement of Pressure Manometer — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
While a barometer measures the pressure of the open atmosphere, this lecture introduces the Manometer, an instrument designed to measure the pressure of enclosed gases (like in a cylinder or a gas pipe).

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher demonstrates a U-shaped tube filled with a liquid (usually water or mercury). One end is connected to the gas supply, and the other end is open to the atmosphere. The difference in the liquid levels in the two arms indicates the difference between the gas pressure and the atmospheric pressure.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Manometer:** A U-tube device used to measure the pressure of enclosed gases relative to atmospheric pressure.
- **Gauge Pressure:** The difference between absolute pressure and atmospheric pressure ($P = 
ho g h$).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- If the liquid is pushed down on the gas side, the gas pressure is greater than atmospheric pressure.
- If the liquid is pushed down on the open side, the atmospheric pressure is greater.
- Gas Pressure = Atmospheric Pressure + $h
ho g$ (if gas side is lower).

## What Students Should Remember
Well, A manometer does not directly give the absolute pressure. It gives the *difference* in pressure ($\Delta P$). Make sure you remember to add the atmospheric pressure back in if calculating total absolute pressure.

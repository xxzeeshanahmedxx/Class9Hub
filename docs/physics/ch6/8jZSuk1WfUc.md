# Exercise Questions — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
In this class, the teacher pivots to review and solve the theoretical Exercise Questions at the end of Chapter 6. This covers MCQs and conceptual reasoning related to stress, strain, density, and pressure.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher walks through the conceptual questions, requiring students to synthesize multiple rules. The teacher explains why wooden blocks float while iron nails sink (density comparison), why high-heeled shoes sink into mud compared to flat shoes (area vs. pressure), and how a barometer accurately reflects altitude changes.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Conceptual Reasoning:** Using the formulas (like $P=F/A$) to justify real-world physical behavior without necessarily doing the math.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Differentiating between Strain (no units) and Stress (N/m²).
- Reviewing the rules of floating and sinking based on the density of water.
- Identifying Pascal's Law as the operating principle behind hydraulic brakes.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: For questions asking "why" an object sinks or floats, stating "because it is heavier" is incorrect and will lose marks. You guys must explicitly use the word "Density" (e.g., "The iron nail sinks because its density is greater than the density of water").

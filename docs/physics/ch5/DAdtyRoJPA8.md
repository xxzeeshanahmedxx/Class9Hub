# Energy, Potential Energy — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This video covers the second primary form of mechanical energy: Potential Energy. The teacher explains how objects can store energy simply by changing their position or physical shape, even if they aren't currently moving.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir defines Potential Energy (P.E.) as stored energy. The lecture focuses primarily on Gravitational Potential Energy—the energy stored when lifting an object against Earth's gravity. A brick held above the ground has the potential to fall and do work. The teacher derives the formula $P.E. = mgh$. Elastic Potential Energy (like in a stretched rubber band) is also briefly mentioned.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Potential Energy (P.E.):** Energy possessed by a body due to its position, state, or configuration.
- **Gravitational Potential Energy:** Energy possessed by a body due to its height above the ground.
- **Elastic Potential Energy:** Stored energy in a stretched or compressed object.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Formula: $P.E. = mgh$ (Mass $\times$ Gravity $\times$ Height).
- Measured in Joules (J).
- Work done in lifting a body ($W = F \times S$) translates directly to the P.E. stored ($mg \times h$).

## What Students Should Remember
Well, Gravitational Potential Energy is always relative to a reference point (usually the ground, where $h=0$ and therefore $P.E.=0$). The higher an object is lifted, the more energy it stores mathematically.

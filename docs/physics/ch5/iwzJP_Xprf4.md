# Work, Energy and Power — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lesson introduces Chapter 5, focusing on teh physics definition of Work. The teacher explains that while we use the word "work" for mental tasks (like studying), physics has a strictly mathematical definition involving physical movement and force.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir defines Work as the product of force and the displacement of an object in the direction of that force. If you push a wall and it doesn't move, you've done zero work in physics, despite getting tired. The teacher introduces the formula $W = F \times S$.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Work ($W$):** Done when a force acting on a body displaces it in the direction of the force.
- **Joule (J):** The SI unit of work. One Joule is the amount of work done when a force of 1 Newton displaces a body through 1 meter.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Work is a Scalar quantity (it has no direction, only magnitude).
- Formula: $W = F \times S$.
- If the force is applied at an angle, the formula becomes $W = F \cos(\theta) \times S$.
- Zero displacement = Zero Work.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: The concept of the angle $\theta$ is crucial. If a force is applied perfectly perpendicular ($90^{\circ}$) to the direction of motion, no work is done cuz $\cos(90^{\circ}) = 0$. This is why carrying a heavy bag horizontally does no work against gravity.

# Numerical Problems 5.1, 5.2, 5.3 — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This session begins the mathematical application of Chapter 5. The teacher solves the first three numerical problems, which focus heavily on calculating Work and Kinetic/Potential Energy.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher reinforces the standard numerical structure. The teacher demonstrates how to apply $W = F \times S$, showing how to handle data where force and displacement are given directly. The lecture then shifts to calculating energy using $K.E. = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$ and $P.E. = mgh$. 

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Substitution Strategy:** Carefully placing given numerical values into the correct algebraic formula to find the unknown.
- **Squaring Velocity:** The critical step in the K.E. formula where the speed must be mathematically squared before halving the mass.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Always ensure mass is in kilograms and velocity is in m/s before using energy formulas.
- In $P.E. = mgh$, assume $g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2$ unless stated otherwise.
- Energy and Work share the exact same unit: Joules (J).

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, A very common arithmetic error occurs in the Kinetic Energy formula. Students often forget to square the velocity ($v^2$) or they square the entire ($mv$) term by mistake. The square applies *only* to the velocity!

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Algebraic Rearrangement:** Shifting variables in a formula to solve for an unknown (e.g., finding $v$ from $K.E. = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$).
- **Percentage Efficiency:** The final step of multiplying the output/input ratio by 100.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Rearranging K.E.: $v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times K.E.}{m}}$.
- Ensure Input and Output are in the same units (e.g., both in Joules or both in Watts) before dividing for efficiency.
- An efficiency of 0.75 is written as 75%.

## What Students Should Remember
Well, When solving for velocity using Kinetic Energy, do not forget to take the square root at the very end of the calculation. Many students calculate $v^2$ correctly but forget the final square root step, losing marks.

# Efficiency — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lesson covers Efficiency, a crucial concept in physics and mechanical engineering. The teacher explains why no machine in the real world is 100% perfect and where the "lost" energy goes.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir explains that whenever you put energy (Input) into a machine, you get useful work (Output) out of it. However, bcuz of friction, heat, and sound, Output is always less than Input. Efficiency is the mathematical ratio comparing the useful output to the total input, usually expressed as a percentage.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Input:** The total energy given to a machine.
- **Useful Output:** The useful work done by the machine.
- **Efficiency:** The ratio of useful work output to the total energy input.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Formula: $\text{Efficiency} = \frac{\text{Useful Output}}{\text{Total Input}} \times 100\%$.
- Efficiency has no unit (it is a pure ratio/percentage).
- An "ideal" machine has 100% efficiency, but this is impossible in reality due to the dissipative effect of friction.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, You guys must remember that the Output can *never* be greater than the Input, as that would violate the Law of Conservation of Energy. If calculating an efficiency greater than 100%, the math is incorrect.

# Use of Permanent Magnets — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This brief lecture focuses on the practical, everyday applications of Permanent Magnets. The teacher highlights how the properties learned in previous lectures are utilized in common engineering and consumer products.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher reviews that permanent magnets are made of hard magnetic materials (like steel or special alloys). Because they do not lose their magnetism easily, they are used in devices that require a constant, reliable magnetic field without needing an electrical power source.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Permanent Magnet Application:** The use of hard magnetic materials to perform a continuous physical task without external energy input.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Compasses:** The needle is a permanent magnet aligning with Earth's magnetic field.
- **Loudspeakers & Headphones:** Use permanent magnets interacting with electric coils to produce sound.
- **Motors:** Small DC motors in toys use permanent magnets for the stator.
- **Everyday tools:** Magnetic door catches, fridge magnets, and magnetic screwdrivers.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, When asked for examples in board exams, students should distinguish between applications of permanent magnets (compasses, fridge doors) and electromagnets (scrap yard cranes, relays). Mixing these up will result in lost marks.

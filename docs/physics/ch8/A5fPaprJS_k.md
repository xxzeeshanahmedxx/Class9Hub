# Magnetisation and Demagnetisation — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the domain theory, this practical lecture covers the methods of Magnetisation and Demagnetisation. The teacher explains the physical processes used to align or scramble the magnetic domains inside a material.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher demonstrates how to magnetize a steel bar using the "Stroking Method" (rubbing a permanent magnet along the bar repeatedly in one direction) or the "Electrical Method" (placing the bar in a DC solenoid). Conversely, the teacher explains how to demagnetize a magnet by scrambling the domains through heating, hammering, or using an Alternating Current (AC) in a solenoid.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Magnetisation:** The process of aligning the magnetic domains in a material to make it a permanent magnet.
- **Demagnetisation:** The process of scrambling the aligned domains to destroy a material's magnetic properties.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Best Magnetisation method:** Placing the steel inside a coil carrying Direct Current (DC).
- **Best Demagnetisation method:** Placing the magnet inside a coil carrying Alternating Current (AC) and slowly withdrawing it in an East-West direction.
- Hitting a magnet hard with a hammer or heating it past its "Curie Temperature" destroys the domain alignment.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, The difference between AC and DC is critical here. Exam questions often ask how to magnetize and demagnetize electrically. You MUST specify Direct Current (DC) for magnetization and Alternating Current (AC) for demagnetization.

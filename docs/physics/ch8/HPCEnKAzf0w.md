# Electromagnets — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Moving from permanent magnets, this lecture introduces Electromagnets. The teacher explains the critical link between electricity and magnetism, a discovery that forms the basis of the modern technological world.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher demonstrates that passing an electric current through a wire creates a magnetic field around it. By coiling the wire into a solenoid and wrapping it around a Soft Iron Core, a powerful electromagnet is created. The teacher explains that this magnet can be turned on and off instantly by controlling the electric current.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Electromagnet:** A temporary magnet made by passing an electric current through a coil of wire surrounding a soft iron core.
- **Solenoid:** A long coil of wire that acts like a bar magnet when a current passes through it.
- **Soft Iron Core:** The material placed inside the coil to magnify the magnetic field strength.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Electromagnets are temporary; no current means no magnetism.
- The strength can be increased by:
  1. Increasing the electric current.
  2. Increasing the number of turns in the coil.
  3. Using a soft iron core instead of wood or plastic.
- Applications: Electric bells, relays, circuit breakers, and heavy lifting cranes.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, The "Soft Iron Core" is a heavily tested concept. Make sure you state that soft iron is used because it magnetizes instantly when the current is on, and demagnetizes completely the moment the current is off, allowing the electromagnet to drop its load.

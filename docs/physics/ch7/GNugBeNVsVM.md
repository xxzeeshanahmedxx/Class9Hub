# Thermometer — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the concept of temperature, this lecture introduces the Thermometer. The teacher explains the physical principles and engineering behind the instruments used to accurately measure the degree of hotness of a body.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher explains that thermometers rely on a "thermometric property"—a physical property that changes linearly with temperature. The most common property is thermal expansion (volume increases as temperature increases). The teacher details the construction of a standard liquid-in-glass thermometer, specifically discussing why mercury or colored alcohol is used as the thermometric liquid.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Thermometer:** An instrument used to measure the temperature of a system.
- **Thermometric Property:** A physical property of a substance that changes uniformly with changes in temperature.
- **Thermal Expansion:** The tendency of matter to change its volume in response to a change in temperature.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Mercury is used because it is opaque, a good conductor of heat, and expands uniformly.
- Alcohol is used in extremely cold regions because it has a much lower freezing point than mercury.
- A thermometer must be calibrated against two fixed points (usually the freezing and boiling points of pure water).

## What Students Should Remember
Well, Students really need to be able to list the properties that make mercury an ideal thermometric liquid. Its uniform expansion and high boiling point are frequently tested in short-answer questions.

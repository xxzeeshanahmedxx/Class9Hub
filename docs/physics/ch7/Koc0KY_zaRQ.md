## Lecture Overview
This session shifts entirely to mathematical application. The teacher solves the numerical problems associated with Chapter 7, focusing predominantly on temperature scale conversions and basic thermodynamic calculations.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher demonstrates the step-by-step application of the conversion formulas. The problems require converting Celsius to Fahrenheit, Fahrenheit to Celsius, and Celsius to Kelvin. The teacher stresses the order of operations (BODMAS), particularly when dealing with the fractions in the Fahrenheit/Celsius equations.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Temperature Conversion:** The mathematical process of translating a thermal reading from one scale into another.
- **Absolute Temperature:** Temperature measured in Kelvin, which must be used for all advanced gas law calculations.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- When converting $C \rightarrow F$: Multiply by $\frac{9}{5}$ first, then add 32.
- When converting $F \rightarrow C$: Subtract 32 first, then multiply by $\frac{5}{9}$.
- When converting $C \rightarrow K$: Simply add 273.

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, The order of operations is the biggest pitfall here. If converting from Fahrenheit to Celsius, you must perform the subtraction inside the brackets $(F - 32)$ *before* you multiply by the fraction. Doing the multiplication first will result in a completely wrong temperature.

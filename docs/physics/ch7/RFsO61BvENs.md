# Temperature Scales — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This highly mathematical lecture covers the three primary Temperature Scales used globally and in science. The teacher explains how to read and convert temperatures between the Celsius, Fahrenheit, and Kelvin scales.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher introduces the fixed points used to create these scales: the freezing point and boiling point of water. The teacher shows that Celsius spans from 0 to 100, Fahrenheit from 32 to 212, and Kelvin from 273 to 373. The lecture focuses heavily on deriving and using the algebraic formulas to convert a temperature reading from one scale to another.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Celsius Scale ($^{\circ}\text{C}$):** The metric temperature scale where water freezes at 0 and boils at 100.
- **Fahrenheit Scale ($^{\circ}\text{F}$):** The imperial temperature scale where water freezes at 32 and boils at 212.
- **Kelvin Scale (K):** The absolute thermodynamic scale, the SI unit of temperature.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Celsius to Kelvin: $T(K) = T(^{\circ}C) + 273$.
- Kelvin to Celsius: $T(^{\circ}C) = T(K) - 273$.
- Celsius to Fahrenheit: $T(^{\circ}F) = \left(T(^{\circ}C) \times \frac{9}{5}\right) + 32$.
- Absolute Zero ($0 \text{ K}$) is the theoretical temperature where all molecular kinetic motion completely stops.

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, The Kelvin scale does not use a degree symbol ($^{\circ}$). Writing "$273^{\circ}\text{K}$" is technically incorrect in physics; it must be written simply as "$273 \text{ K}$". Additionally, converting Celsius to Kelvin is the most vital mathematical skill required for gas law problems.

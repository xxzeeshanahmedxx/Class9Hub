# Centre of Gravity and Centre of Mass — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This conceptual lecture explores the Centre of Gravity (CG) and Centre of Mass (CM). The teacher explains how complex, large bodies can be simplified into a single point for physics calculations.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher defines the Centre of Mass as the point where an applied force causes the body to move in a straight line without spinning. The Centre of Gravity is defined as the exact point where the total weight of the body appears to act vertically downward. For symmetric, regular objects (like a uniform ruler or a sphere), the CG is exactly in the geometric center.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Centre of Mass:** A point where an applied force causes only linear motion without rotation.
- **Centre of Gravity:** A point through which the entire weight of the body acts downward.
- **Symmetric Body:** A body with a uniform geometric shape and even mass distribution.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- In uniform gravitational fields (like on Earth), CG and CM are at the exact same location.
- CG of a circle/sphere is its center.
- CG of a rectangle is the intersection of its diagonals.
- CG of a triangle is the intersection of its medians.

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, Memorizing the location of the Centre of Gravity for different geometric shapes (triangles, squares, cylinders) is a frequent MCQ topic in board exams.

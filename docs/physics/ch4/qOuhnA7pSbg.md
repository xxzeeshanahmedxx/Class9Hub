# Equilibrium — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lesson officially introduces the concept of Equilibrium. The teacher explains the mathematical and physical conditions required for an object to be perfectly balanced and stable in its environment.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher states that an object is in equilibrium if no net force and no net torque act upon it. The lecture covers the two primary types of equilibrium: Static (objects completely at rest, like a book on a table) and Dynamic (objects moving at a constant, uniform velocity, like a paratrooper falling at terminal velocity).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Equilibrium:** A state in wich opposing forces or torques are perfectly balanced.
- **Static Equilibrium:** When a body is completely at rest.
- **Dynamic Equilibrium:** When a body is moving with uniform linear or rotational velocity.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- If Acceleration = 0, the body is in Equilibrium.
- A parked car is in Static Equilibrium.
- A satellite orbiting Earth at constant speed is in Dynamic Equilibrium.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, The biggest misconception is that equilibrium only applies to stationary objects. You guys must remember that moving objects (like cars cruising at a steady 60 km/h) are also in equilibrium because their net acceleration is zero.

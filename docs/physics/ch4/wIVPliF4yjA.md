# Resolution Of Vectors — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
In this highly mathematical lecture, the teacher introduces the Resolution of Vectors. Instead of adding vectors together, this process involves splitting a single diagonal vector into two perpendicular components.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir uses trigonometry to break down a vector (like a force acting at an angle). If you pull a toy car with a string at an angle, part of the force pulls it forward (Horizontal Component, $F_x$) and part pulls it upward (Vertical Component, $F_y$). The teacher derives these components using sine and cosine functions from a right-angled triangle.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Resolution of Vectors:** Splitting a single vector into two mutually perpendicular components.
- **Perpendicular Components:** Two vector components that are at a 90-degree angle to each other ($F_x$ and $F_y$).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- The horizontal component formula: $F_x = F \cos(\theta)$.
- The vertical component formula: $F_y = F \sin(\theta)$.
- $\theta$ is the angle made with the horizontal x-axis.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: Memorizing the trigonometric formulas is non-negotiable. Students realy need to remember that $F_x$ is strictly linked to cosine, and $F_y$ is strictly linked to sine (when the angle is measured from the x-axis). Mixing these up will ruin numerical calculations.

In this wrap-up session, the teacher reviews the theoretical Exercise Questions and MCQs at the end of Chapter 4. Basically, this class consolidates all the concepts of torques, forces, and equilibrium.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir walks through the conceptual queries, explaining how to deduce the right answer using the newly learned rules. The teacher focuses on questions involving the resolution of vectors, identifying states of equilibrium from diagrams, and the conditions required to stop rotation.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Conceptual Synthesis:** Combining multiple physics rules (like Torque and Centre of Gravity) to answer a single question.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Reviewing the formulas: $\tau = F \times L$, $F_x = F \cos(\theta)$, $F_y = F \sin(\theta)$.
- Identifying the Centre of Gravity in various shapes.
- Explaining why door handles are placed far from hinges (maximum moment arm).

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: When answering conceptual questions about torque, always explicitly mention the "Moment Arm." Explaining that an action is easier because it "increases the moment arm, thereby increasing the torque for the same force" guarantees maximum marks.

# Principle of Moments — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This video applies the concept of Torque to balanced systems by introducing the Principle of Moments. The teacher explains how objects like seesaws maintain balance when different weights are placed at different distances from the pivot.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher differentiates between Clockwise Torque (which makes an object spin right) and Anticlockwise Torque (which makes it spin left). The Principle of Moments states that a body is in equilibrium if the sum of clockwise moments perfectly equals the sum of anticlockwise moments.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Principle of Moments:** For a body in equilibrium, the sum of clockwise torques equals the sum of anticlockwise torques about any pivot.
- **Clockwise Torque:** The turning effect that causes rotation in the direction of clock hands.
- **Anticlockwise Torque:** The turning effect that causes rotation opposite to clock hands.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Equation: $\sum \tau_{cw} = \sum \tau_{acw}$.
- A heavier person must sit closer to the pivot to balance a lighter person sitting further away ($F_1 L_1 = F_2 L_2$).
- This principle is the basis for all lever-based simple machines.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: When solving numericals involving the principle of moments, it is critical to measure the Moment Arm ($L$) strictly from the *pivot point*, not from the end of the object.

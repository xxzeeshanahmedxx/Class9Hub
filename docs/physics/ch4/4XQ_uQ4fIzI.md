# Conditions of Equilibrium — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the general concept of equilibrium, this mathematical lecture outlines the strict Conditions of Equilibrium. The teacher details the two algebraic rules an object must satisfy to be considered perfectly balanced.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher introduces the First Condition of Equilibrium: The vector sum of all forces acting on the body must be zero ($\sum F = 0$). This prevents linear motion. However, forces can cancel out linearly but still cause spinning. Therefore, the Second Condition of Equilibrium is required: The vector sum of all torques must be zero ($\sum \tau = 0$), preventing rotational motion.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **First Condition of Equilibrium:** Net force is zero ($\sum F_x = 0$ and $\sum F_y = 0$).
- **Second Condition of Equilibrium:** Net torque is zero ($\sum \tau = 0$).
- **Sigma ($\sum$):** A mathematical symbol meaning "the sum of".

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- To be in complete equilibrium, a body must satisfy BOTH conditions simultaneously.
- A couple (like hands on a steering wheel) satisfies the first condition (net force is zero) but violates the second (it causes rotation).
- The Principle of Moments is an application of the second condition.

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, Examiners frequently ask if a body can be in equilibrium if only the first condition is satisfied. The answer is NO. The steering wheel example perfectly illustrates why the second condition (zero torque) is mandatory to prevent spinning.

# Determination of a Force from its Perpendicular Components — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following vector resolution, this lecture teaches the reverse process: determining a single resultant force from its perpendicular $x$ and $y$ components.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
If you are given $F_x$ and $F_y$, you can find the original force ($F$) and its exact angle ($\theta$). The teacher uses the Pythagorean theorem to find the magnitude of the force ($F = \sqrt{F_x^2 + F_y^2}$). To find the angle (direction), the instructor uses the trigonometric tangent ratio ($\tan(\theta) = \frac{F_y}{F_x}$).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Magnitude:** The numerical value or strength of the resultant vector.
- **Direction:** The exact angle ($\theta$) at wich the resultant force acts relative to the horizontal axis.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Magnitude Formula: $F = \sqrt{F_x^2 + F_y^2}$.
- Direction Formula: $\theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{F_y}{F_x}\right)$.
- This process is the exact mathematical inverse of resolving vectors.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: When calculating direction, you must use the inverse tangent ($\tan^{-1}$) on the calculator. Furthermore, a common mistake is putting $F_x$ on top; it must strictly be $\frac{F_y}{F_x}$ to get the correct angle with the horizontal.

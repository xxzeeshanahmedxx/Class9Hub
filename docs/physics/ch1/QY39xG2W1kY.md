# Exercise MCQ's Chapter No 1 — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
In this class, the teacher shifts focus from theory to examination practice by solving the textbook Exercise Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) for Chapter 1. This session acts as a rapid review, helping students apply the concepts they've learned to specific exam-style scenarios.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher goes through the MCQs sequentially. Instead of just giving the answers, the teacher takes the time to explain *why* the correct option is right and why the other options are wrong. The questions heavily feature topics like identifying base vs. derived quantities, recognizing correct SI units, determining least counts of instruments, and applying the rules of significant figures.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **MCQ (Multiple Choice Question):** An assessment item where students select the correct answer from a list of options.
- **Distractor:** The incorrect options in an MCQ designed to test true comprehension.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
Key MCQ themes discussed:
- Identifying strictly the 7 Base Quantities (Length, Mass, Time, etc.).
- Verifying the least count of the Vernier Calliper (0.1 mm) and Screw Gauge (0.01 mm).
- Selecting the correct conversion of prefixes (e.g., micro $= 10^{-6}$, milli $= 10^{-3}$).
- Applying rounding rules and finding significant figures in tricky decimal numbers.

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, Memorizing answers is a bad strategy. Students should focus on the logical deduction the teacher uses to eliminate wrong options. For instance, quickly recalling that area is derived (length $\times$ length) helps instantly eliminate it if a question asks for a base quantity.

# International System of Unit — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Here,, stdents are introduced to the International System of Units (SI). The teacher explains the historical need for a standardized global measurement system to ensure scientific and commercial consistency worldwide. This topic forms the basis for all quantitative physics problems in the 9th class syllabus.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The primary focus is on why local units were discarded in favor of a universal standard. The teacher explains the 11th General Conference on Weights and Measures (1960), which established the SI system. The lecture differentiates between SI base units (like meters for length and kilograms for mass) and SI derived units (like meters per second for speed).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **SI Units:** A globally accepted system of physical units.
- **Base Units:** The fundamental units used to measure base quantities (e.g., kg, m, s, A, K, cd, mol).
- **Derived Units:** Units obtained by multiplying or dividing base units (e.g., m/s, N, J).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- The SI system was adopted globally in 1960.
- 7 Base SI Units: Meter (Length), Kilogram (Mass), Second (Time), Ampere (Current), Kelvin (Temperature), Candela (Intensity of Light), Mole (Amount of substance).
- Derived units depend entirely on base units.

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, Always write numerical answers with their correct SI units; an answer without a unit is considered incomplete in physics exams.

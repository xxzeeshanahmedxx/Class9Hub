# Mass Measuring Instruments Physical Balance — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Shifting from length to mass, this lecture focuses on Mass Measuring Instruments, specifically the Physical Balance. The teacher outlines how mass is quantified in laboratories and everyday life, comparing traditional beam balances to more accurate physical balances.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher begins with older tools like the simple beam balance used by shopkeepers. The lesson then transitions to the Physical Balance used in physic labs. The teacher explains the principle of moments, where unknown mass on one pan is balanced by standard mass weights on the other. The lecture details the balancing process, including using the leveling screws and the plumb line to ensure horizontal alignment before measurement.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Mass:** The quantity of matter contained in a body.
- **Physical Balance:** A highly sensitive laboratory instrument used to measure the mass of an object by comparison.
- **Standard Weights:** Precise masses kept in a weight box used for comparison.
- **Plumb Line:** A string with a weight used to ensure the balance pillar is perfectly vertical.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Physical balances measure mass, not weight.
- Always check the resting position of the pointer at the zero mark before measuring.
- Keep the balance doors closed to prevent air currents from affecting the reading.
- Handle standard weights with forceps, never with bare hands.

## What Students Should Remember
Well, The most common mistake is confusing mass and weight. The Physical Balance measures mass (in grams/kilograms) by comparing it directly to standard masses, completely independent of local gravity.

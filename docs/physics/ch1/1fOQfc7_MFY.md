# Length Measuring Instrument — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This 9th class lecture introduces practical physics by discussing basic Length Measuring Instruments. The teacher focuses on the Meter Rule and Measuring Tape, explaining their construction, proper usage, and limitations in scientific measurements.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher begins by showing a standard Meter Rule, explaining that it is exactly one meter long and divided into 100 centimeters, which are further divided into millimeters. The concept of "Least Count" is introduced here—the smallest value an instrument can measure accurately. For a meter rule, this is 1 mm. The teacher also demonstrates how to avoid "parallax error" by looking straight down at the scale.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Meter Rule:** A one-meter long wooden or metal scale used for general length measurements.
- **Measuring Tape:** A flexible tape used by carpenters and surveyors for lengths greater than 1 meter.
- **Least Count:** The minimum measurement that can be accurately read on an instrument.
- **Parallax Error:** A visual error caused by reading a scale from an incorrect angle.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Meter Rule Least Count: 1 mm (or 0.1 cm).
- Always place the eye vertically above the reading mark.
- Measuring tapes come in 10m, 20m, 50m, and 100m sizes.

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, Make sure you memorize the least count of the meter rule (1 mm). It is a frequent multiple-choice question (MCQ) in 9th class exams. Additionally, understanding parallax error is vital for laboratory practicals.

# Vernier Callipers — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This important lecture covers the Vernier Calliper, an instrument used to measure lengths with greater precision than a meter rule. The teacher explains its components, working principles, and how to take accurate readings for both internal and external diameters.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher details the construction of the Vernier Calliper, highlighting the fixed Main Scale and the movable Vernier Scale. Because a meter rule can only measure down to 1 mm, the Vernier Calliper is introduced to measure down to 0.1 mm. The teacher thoroughly explains how to calculate its Least Count by dividing the smallest main scale division by the total number of divisions on the Vernier scale.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Vernier Calliper:** A precision measuring instrument used to measure up to 0.1 mm.
- **Main Scale:** The fixed scale, usually marked in centimeters and millimeters.
- **Vernier Scale:** The sliding scale that provides precision.
- **Vernier Constant (Least Count):** The difference between one main scale division and one vernier scale division (0.1 mm).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Components: Outside jaws, inside jaws, depth probe, locking screw.
- Least Count formula: $\frac{\text{Smallest reading on main scale}}{\text{Total divisions on vernier scale}} = \frac{1 \text{mm}}{10} = 0.1 \text{mm}$ (or 0.01 cm).
- Total Reading = Main Scale Reading + (Vernier Scale Coinciding Division $\times$ Least Count).

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, The Least Count of a Vernier Calliper in both mm (0.1 mm) and cm (0.01 cm) is heavily tested. Students really need to understand how to find the coinciding line on the Vernier scale to calculate the exact measurement during physics practicals.

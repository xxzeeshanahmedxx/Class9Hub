# Force Meter (Spring Balance) — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Continuing the discussion on measuring instruments, this lecture focuses specifically on the Force Meter, commonly known as a Spring Balance. This tool is uniquely designed to measure forces, including weight, directly in Newtons.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher demonstrates the Spring Balance, explaining that it operates on Hooke's Law (the extension of the spring is directly proportional to the applied force). Our teacher shows how suspending a mass stretches the spring, and a pointer indicates the force on a calibrated scale.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Force Meter / Spring Balance:** An instrument that uses a coiled spring to measure force or weight.
- **Hooke's Law:** The physical principle stating that a spring's stretch is proportional to the pull force.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Spring balances explicitly measure Force (Weight), not Mass.
- The scale is usually graduated in Newtons (N).
- If taken to the moon, the spring balance would show a lower reading for the exact same object.

## What Students Should Remember
Well, The Spring Balance is the classic exception in measuring tools. You guys must remember that it measures weight (vector) in Newtons, unlike a physical beam balance that measures mass (scalar) in kilograms.

# Mechanical and Electronic Balances — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Building on mass and weight, this lecture reviews the instruments used to measure these quantities. The teacher compares Mechanical Balances with modern Electronic Balances, explaining their mechanisms and accuracy.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher explains that beam balances and physical balances (mechanical) measure *mass* by comparing an unknown object to standard weights. The teacher then demonstrates an electronic balance, which measures the downward force (weight) using internal sensors but is calibrated to display the reading in mass (grams/kilograms). 

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Mechanical Balance:** An instrument that measures mass through physical comparison of moments.
- **Electronic Balance:** A highly precise digital instrument that uses internal load cells to calculate mass.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Mechanical balances work independently of gravity variations because gravity pulls on both sides equally.
- Electronic balances are much faster, more precise, and have a smaller least count (e.g., 0.001g).
- Always "tare" (zero) an electronic balance before measuring to avoid zero errors.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: While electronic balances give a reading in kilograms, their internal mechanism technically measures force (weight) and divides by $g$. Therefore, they must be calibrated to local gravity to be perfectly accurate.

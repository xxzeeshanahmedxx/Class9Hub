# Newton's 3rd Law & Limitation of Newton's Law of Motion — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lesson concludes Newton's Laws by discussing the Third Law of Motion (Action and Reaction). The teacher also explores the boundaries of classical physics by discussing the limitations of Newton's laws.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir states the law: To every action, there is always an equal and opposite reaction. The teacher emphasizes that action and reaction forces *always act on two different bodies*, which is why they do not cancel each other out. Later, the teacher briefly explains that Newton's laws fail at the microscopic quantum level or at speeds close to the speed of light.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Newton's Third Law:** For every action force, there is an equal and opposite reaction force.
- **Action/Reaction Pairs:** Forces that occur simultaneously between two interacting objects.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Action and Reaction are equal in magnitude.
- Action and Reaction are opposite in direction.
- Examples: Rocket propulsion, walking on the ground, firing a bullet from a gun.
- Limitation: Newtonian mechanics breaks down for subatomic particles (Quantum Mechanics) and light-speed objects (Relativity).

## What Students Should Remember
Well, The most common mistake is thinking action and reaction cancel out. They do not cancel because they act on *different* objects. For example, a foot pushes the earth (action), and the earth pushes the foot forward (reaction).

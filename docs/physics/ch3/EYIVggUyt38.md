# Newton's First Law of Motion — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This critical lecture covers Newton's First Law of Motion, also known as the Law of Inertia. The teacher explains the natural tendency of objects to resist any change in their current state.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher states the law: A body at rest remains at rest, and a body in motion continues to move with uniform velocity, unless acted upon by an unbalanced net force. The teacher introduces "Inertia," explaining that heavier objects have more inertia and are thus harder to move or stop.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Newton's First Law:** An object continues its state of rest or uniform motion unless an unbalanced force acts on it.
- **Inertia:** The property of a body to resist any change in its state of rest or motion.
- **Net Force:** The sum of all vector forces acting on a body.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Inertia is directly proportional to mass.
- If Net Force $= 0$, acceleration $= 0$.
- Examples of Inertia: Passengers falling forward when a bus suddenly brakes.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: Newton’s First Law only applies when there is *zero net force*. Make sure you memorize that "Inertia depends only on mass." A moving bicycle and a stationary truck both have inertia, but the truck has more becuz of its mass.

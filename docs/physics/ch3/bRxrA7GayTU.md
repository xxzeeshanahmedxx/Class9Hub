# Fundamental forces — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Basically, this class delves deeper into Non-Contact forces by exploring the Fundamental Forces of Nature. The teacher explains the four basic interactions that govern the entire universe, from microscopic atoms to massive galaxies.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir outlines the four fundamental forces: Gravitational, Electromagnetic, Strong Nuclear, and Weak Nuclear. The teacher explains that gravity acts on mass, electromagnetism acts on charge, and the nuclear forces act inside atoms. The relative strengths and ranges of these forces are compared.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Gravitational Force:** The weakest fundamental force, acting over infinite distances between masses.
- **Electromagnetic Force:** The force between charged particles; responsible for everyday pushes/pulls.
- **Strong Nuclear Force:** The strongest force in nature, holding atomic nuclei together.
- **Weak Nuclear Force:** The force responsible for radioactive decay.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Strongest to weakest: Strong Nuclear > Electromagnetic > Weak Nuclear > Gravitational.
- Gravity and Electromagnetism have infinite ranges.
- Nuclear forces only work at microscopic, sub-atomic distances.

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, It is a common exam trick: Gravity is the weakest force in the universe, despite being responsible for holding planets in orbit. The Strong Nuclear force is the strongest but has the shortest range.

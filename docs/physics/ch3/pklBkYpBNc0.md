# Concept of force & Types of force — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Chapter 3 shifts from Kinematics to Dynamics. This introductory lecture focuses on the core concept of Dynamics: Force. The teacher explains that while Chapter 2 discussed how objects move, this chapter explores exactly *why* they move.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher defines force as an agent that produces or tends to produce motion, or stops or tends to stop motion. Force can also change the direction or shape of an object. The teacher then breaks force down into two main types: Contact Forces (which require physical touching) and Non-Contact Forces (which act over a distance).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Dynamics:** The branch of mechanics dealing with forces and their effect on motion.
- **Force:** A push or pull that changes an object's state of rest or motion.
- **Contact Force:** Forces that act on objects through direct physical contact.
- **Non-Contact Force:** Forces that act without direct contact, usually through a field.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- SI Unit of force is the Newton (N).
- Force is a vector quantity (magnitude + direction).
- Examples of Contact Forces: Friction, Tension, Normal force.
- Examples of Non-Contact Forces: Gravity, Magnetic force, Electrostatic force.

## What Students Should Remember
Look, Force doesn't always cause movement; it "tends to" cause it. Pushing a concrete wall is applying a force, even if the wall doesn't move.

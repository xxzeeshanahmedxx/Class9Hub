## Lecture Overview
This session begins the mathematical application of Chapter 3. The teacher solves the first four numerical problems, focusing on Newton's Second Law and the relationship between weight and mass.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir reinforces the standard structure: Data, Required, Formula, and Solution. The problems primarily involve applying $F = ma$ and $W = mg$. The teacher demonstrates how to calculate mass when weight is given, and then how to use that mass to find the acceleration produced by a specific force.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Data Extraction:** Identifying $F$, $m$, $a$, and $W$ from a word problem.
- **Substitution:** Replacing variables with their numerical values in a physics formula.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Always ensure mass is in kg and force is in Newtons before calculating.
- If an object is moving vertically, weight ($W$) is the primary force acting on it.
- A common pattern is finding mass using $m = \frac{W}{g}$, and then substituting $m$ into $F = ma$.

## What Students Should Remember
Well, Make sure you not mix up Mass ($m$) and Weight ($W$) in numericals. If a problem states "A body of weight 20N," do NOT plug 20 into the mass variable in $F=ma$. You must divide 20 by 10 (gravity) to get 2kg first.

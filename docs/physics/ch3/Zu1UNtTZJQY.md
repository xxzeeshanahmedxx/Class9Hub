# Friction (Terminal Velocity, Rolling Friction) — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lecture continues the topic of friction, expanding it to Rolling Friction and fluid friction (Air Resistance). The teacher also explains the concept of Terminal Velocity, a key phenomenon in free-fall dynamics.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher explains that a rolling wheel experiences Rolling Friction, which is significantly less than sliding friction because the contact area is much smaller and cold welds are constantly broken rather than dragged. The lecture then transitions to air resistance acting on a falling object. As an object falls faster, air friction increases until it exactly balances gravity, resulting in Terminal Velocity.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Rolling Friction:** The resistance an object encounters when it rolls over a surface.
- **Air Resistance (Drag):** Fluid friction acting on an object moving through the air.
- **Terminal Velocity:** The constant, maximum velocity reached by a falling object when air resistance perfectly balances the gravitational force.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Rolling friction < Sliding friction < Static friction.
- This is why ball bearings and wheels are used in machinery.
- At terminal velocity, Net Force = 0, so Acceleration = 0.
- Parachutes increase surface area to lower terminal velocity to a safe speed.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: Terminal velocity is a perfect example of Newton's First Law. Even though the object is falling, because the downward weight equals the upward air friction, the net force is zero, and it falls at a constant, uniform speed.

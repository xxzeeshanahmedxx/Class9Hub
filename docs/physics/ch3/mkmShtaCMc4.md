# Exercise Questions — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
In this class, the teacher shifts to examination preparation, solving the theoretical Exercise Questions at the end of Chapter 3. This includes MCQs and conceptual short answers related to dynamics.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher reviews Newton's laws, friction, and momentum by answering the textbook's conceptual queries. The teacher explains the physical reasoning behind why certain choices are correct, emphasizing the difference between mass and weight, and the application of $F=ma$ in multiple-choice scenarios.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Conceptual Justification:** Applying a physics law (like Conservation of Momentum) to logically explain a textbook scenario.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Reviewing the units: Force (N), Mass (kg), Momentum (N$\cdot$s or kg$\cdot$m/s).
- Identifying action-reaction pairs in various examples.
- Explaining why friction is a necessary evil (e.g., walking, braking).

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, Memorizing the textbook answers is less important than understanding the *why*. When a conceptual question asks why passengers fall forward when a bus stops, students must explicitly use the keyword "Inertia of motion" to score full marks.

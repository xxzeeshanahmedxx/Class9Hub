# Momentum and Impulse — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Basically, this class introduces Momentum and Impulse, two concepts that deal with mass in motion and the impact of forces over time. These are essential for understanding collisions and safety mechanics.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher defines Momentum as the "quantity of motion" contained in a body, mathematically calculated as mass multiplied by velocity ($p = mv$). A heavy truck moving slowly can have the same momentum as a small bullet moving vry fast. The lecture then introduces Impulse, which is a large force applied over a very short time interval, resulting in a change in momentum.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Momentum ($p$):** The product of a body's mass and its velocity. It is a vector quantity.
- **Impulse ($I$ or $J$):** The product of force and the time duration it is applied. It equals the change in momentum ($\Delta p$).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Formula for Momentum: $p = mv$.
- Unit of Momentum: kg$\cdot$m/s (or N$\cdot$s).
- Formula for Impulse: $I = F \times \Delta t = \Delta p$.
- Safety features like airbags and seatbelts work by increasing the time of impact ($\Delta t$), thereby decreasing the deadly force ($F$).

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, Understanding the relationship between Force and Momentum is vital: Force is the *rate of change* of momentum ($F = \frac{\Delta p}{t}$). This is actually the original, more accurate form of Newton's Second Law.

# Principle of Conservation of Momentum — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This crucial lecture covers the Principle of Conservation of Momentum. The teacher explores how momentum behaves in a closed, isolated system when two or more objects collide.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher states the law: In an isolated system (where no external forces like friction act), the total momentum before a collision is exactly equal to the total momentum after the collision. The teacher demonstrates this mathematically using the classic example of a gun firing a bullet (recoil).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Law of Conservation of Momentum:** The total momentum of an isolated system remains constant.
- **Isolated System:** A collection of interacting objects where no net external unbalanced force acts.
- **Recoil:** The backward momentum of a gun when it fires a bullet forward.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Initial total momentum = Final total momentum.
- Equation for two colliding bodies: $m_1 u_1 + m_2 u_2 = m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2$.
- The gun recoils because the bullet gains forward momentum, and the system must maintain a total momentum of zero (since both were initially at rest).

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: The recoil of a gun is a highly tested exam question. Because the gun's mass ($M$) is much larger than the bullet's mass ($m$), the recoil velocity of the gun is much smaller than the bullet's forward velocity.

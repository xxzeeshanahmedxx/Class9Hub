# Interdisciplinary Nature of Physics — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This lesson explores the Interdisciplinary Nature of Physics. The teacher explains that the hard lines between science subjects are blurring. Physics frequently merges with other sciences to solve complex modern problems.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher introduces "overlapping" fields. When physics principles are applied to biological systems (like the mechanics of a beating heart), it creates Biophysics. When physics is used to understand chemical bonds and reactions at a quantum level, it becomes Physical Chemistry. The lecture highlights how physics acts as the mathematical backbone for other sciences.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Interdisciplinary:** Involving two or more academic, scientific, or artistic disciplines.
- **Biophysics:** The application of physics principles and methods to biological problems.
- **Astrophysics:** The branch of astronomy that uses physics to understand the universe.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Geophysics:** Studying the Earth's internal structure (earthquakes, magnetic fields) using physics.
- **Medical Physics:** Using physics for healthcare (X-rays, MRI machines, radiation therapy).
- No single science can exist entirely on its own anymore; they all rely on physical laws.

## What Students Should Remember
So basically, Students really need to be able to define the interdisciplinary branches. A classic exam question will ask to name the branch of science that deals with the physical properties of the Earth—the correct answer is Geophysics.

# Branches of Physics — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Because the scope of physics is so massive, it must be categorized. This video introduces the different Branches of Physics, organizing the subject into distinct fields of study based on the phenomena being investigated.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher categorizes physics into Classical Physics (pre-20th century) and Modern Physics. The teacher lists and defines the major branches, including Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Electromagnetism, Optics, and Atomic/Nuclear Physics. Each branch is explained with a brief real-world example to provide context.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Mechanics:** The study of motion and the forces that cause it.
- **Thermodynamics:** The study of heat and its transformation into other forms of energy.
- **Optics:** The study of light and its behavior.
- **Nuclear Physics:** The study of the atomic nucleus and its interactions.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- **Classical Physics Branches:** Mechanics, Heat, Sound, Light, Electricity.
- **Modern Physics Branches:** Quantum Mechanics, Relativity, Particle Physics, Astrophysics.
- Modern physics deals with the extreme: either incredibly fast (speed of light) or incredibly small (atoms).

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: Questions regarding the branches of physics are very common MCQs. Make sure you be able to match the branch with its definition (e.g., identifying that the study of falling objects belongs to Mechanics, while studying a telescope belongs to Optics).

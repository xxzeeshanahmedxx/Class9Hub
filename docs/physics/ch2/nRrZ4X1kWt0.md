# Numerical Problems 2.1 To 2.5 — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This session marks the transition from theory to mathematical application. The teacher solves the first five numerical problems of Chapter 2, demonstrating the step-by-step methodology required to score full marks in board exams.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher emphasizes the "Given, Required, Formula, Solution" structure. The problems cover basic speed, velocity, and introductory acceleration. The teacher spends significant time showing how to extract hidden data from the text, such as recognizing that "starts from rest" mathematically means initial velocity ($v_i$) $= 0$.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Data Extraction:** The skill of reading a word problem and identifying the known and unknown physical variables.
- **Unit Conversion:** Changing given units (like km/h) into standard SI units (m/s) before applying formulas.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Always convert km/h to m/s by multiplying by $\frac{1000}{3600}$.
- If an object comes to a stop, $v_f = 0$.
- Write down the three equations of motion in the margins before starting.

## What Students Should Remember
Honestly, Students really need to rigorously follow the teacher’s formatting. Skipping the data extraction step or failing to convert units before plugging them into the equation of motion is the leading cause of lost marks in numerical sections.

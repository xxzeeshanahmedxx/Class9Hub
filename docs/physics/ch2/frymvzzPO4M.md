# Kinematics Acceleration — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
This video tackles Acceleration, explaining what happens when the velocity of an object is not constant. This is a vital concept for understanding real-world driving, gravity, and forces.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Sir defines acceleration as the rate of change of velocity. If a car speeds up, slows down, or changes direction, it is accelerating. The lecture covers the formula ($a = \frac{v_f - v_i}{t}$) and distinguishes between positive acceleration (speeding up) and negative acceleration/retardation (slowing down).

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Acceleration ($a$):** The rate of change of velocity with respect to time.
- **Retardation / Deceleration:** Negative acceleration; when the velocity of a body is decreasing.
- **Uniform Acceleration:** Changing velocity by equal amounts in equal time intervals.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- SI Unit is meters per second squared (m/s²).
- Acceleration is a vector quantity.
- Direction of positive acceleration is the same as velocity. Direction of negative acceleration is opposite to velocity.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: Make sure you memorize the unit (m/s²) and remember that when brakes are applied, the final velocity ($v_f$) is smaller than the initial velocity ($v_i$), mathematically resulting in a negative acceleration.

# Speed Time Graph — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following distance-time graphs, this lecture introduces Speed-Time Graphs. This is a more complex graph that allows students to extract two distinct physical quantities simultaneously: acceleration and distance.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher plots Time on the x-axis and Speed on the y-axis. Our teacher reveals two golden rules: First, the slope (gradient) of a speed-time graph gives the acceleration. Second, the total area enclosed under the graph line mathematically equals the total distance traveled by the object. 

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Speed-Time Graph:** A graph showing how an object's speed changes over time.
- **Area under the curve:** The geometric area formed between the plotted line and the time axis, representing total distance.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Horizontal line on a speed-time graph = Constant uniform speed (zero acceleration).
- Straight diagonal line going up = Uniform positive acceleration.
- Straight diagonal line going down = Uniform deceleration (retardation).
- Distance = Area of rectangle ($L \times W$) or triangle ($\frac{1}{2} \times B \times H$) under the graph.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: Do not confuse the graphs! A horizontal line on a *distance-time* graph means the object is stopped. But a horizontal line on a *speed-time* graph means the object is moving constantly without accelerating. Furthermore, finding distance via the area under the graph is a guaranteed exam question.

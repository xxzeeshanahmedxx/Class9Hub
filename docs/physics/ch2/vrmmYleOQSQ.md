# Kinematics Speed & Velocity — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Building on distance and displacement, this lecture introduces Speed and Velocity. The teacher explains the rate at which an object covers spatial ground, distinguishing between scalar and vector rates of motion.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Speed is defined as the distance covered per unit of time, making it a scalar quantity. Velocity is the rate of change of displacement, making it a vector quantity. The teacher also introduces the concepts of uniform (constant) speed/velocity versus variable speed/velocity.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Speed ($v$):** Distance covered in unit time ($v = \frac{S}{t}$).
- **Velocity ($\vec{v}$):** Displacement in unit time ($\vec{v} = \frac{\vec{d}}{t}$).
- **Uniform Velocity:** Covering equal displacement in equal intervals of time.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- SI Unit for both is meters per second (m/s or ms$^{-1}$).
- Speed tells you how fast; velocity tells you how fast *and* in what direction.
- Changing direction changes velocity, even if the speed remains constant (e.g., circular motion).

## What Students Should Remember
Well, You guys must understand that since velocity is a vector, an object moving in a circle at a steady 50 km/h has a constant speed, but a variable velocity because its direction is continuously changing.

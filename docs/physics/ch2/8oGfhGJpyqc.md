# Numerical Problems 2.6 To 2.10 — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Here,, the teacher solves the final five numerical problems of Chapter 2. These are the advanced, multi-step problems that frequently appear as long questions in exams, focusing heavily on the equations of motion and gravity.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
Our teacher tackles complex scenarios, such as trains decelerating and objects thrown upwards. The teacher explains how to choose the *correct* equation of motion based on the available data. For example, if time ($t$) is not given, the 3rd equation ($2aS = v_f^2 - v_i^2$) is the only viable choice. 

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Equations of Motion:** The three algebraic formulas relating displacement, initial velocity, final velocity, acceleration, and time.
- **Simultaneous Application:** Using one equation to find an intermediate variable (like time), to then use a second equation to find the final answer.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Problem 2.6 requires applying deceleration correctly.
- Problems 2.8 and 2.9 involve motion under gravity, requiring the $g = \pm 10$ sign convention.
- Always check if the final answer makes logical sense (e.g., stopping distance cannot be negative).

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: The strategic selection of the formula is the hardest part. Students should remember to list the variables they have ($v_i, a, S$) and look for the equation that contains only one unknown variable.

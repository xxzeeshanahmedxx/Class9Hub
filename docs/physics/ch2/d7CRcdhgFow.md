# Rest And Motion — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Here,, the core concepts of Rest and Motion are explored. The teacher explains that these are relative terms, depending entirely on the perspective of the observer (the frame of reference).

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher uses the classic example of a passenger in a moving bus. To a person standing outside, the passenger is in motion. To another passenger sitting inside the same bus, the first passenger is at rest. This proves that absolute rest or motion does not exist.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Rest:** A body is at rest if it does not change its position with respect to its surroundings.
- **Motion:** A body is in motion if it changes its position with respect to its surroundings.
- **Surroundings:** The objects in the immediate environment acting as a reference point.

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- Rest and motion are always relative.
- The state of a body is defined by the observer's frame of reference.
- Earth itself is in constant motion, so nothing on Earth is at "absolute rest".

## What Students Should Remember
Actually, When defining rest or motion in exams, students must include the phrase "with respect to its surroundings," otherwise the definition is incomplete and scientifically inaccurate.

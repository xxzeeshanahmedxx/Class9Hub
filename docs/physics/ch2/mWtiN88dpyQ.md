# Representation Of Vectors — Class 9 Physics Lecture Summary

## Lecture Overview
Following the introduction to vectors, this lecture covers the Representation of Vectors. Since vectors have direction, they require special graphical and symbolic notation compared to standard numbers.

## Main Concepts Explained by the Teacher
The teacher shows two ways to represent vectors: symbolically and graphically. Symbolically, a vector is written with a bold letter or an arrow over it ($\vec{A}$). Graphically, it is represented by a straight line with an arrowhead. The length of the line represents the magnitude (using a scale), and the arrowhead points in the direction.

## Important Definitions and Terms
- **Symbolic Representation:** Using boldface letters or an over-arrow.
- **Graphical Representation:** Using a scaled straight line with an arrowhead.
- **Scale:** A mathematical ratio used to represent large magnitudes on a small piece of paper (e.g., 1 cm = 10 N).

## Key Points and Lists from the Lecture
- The tail of the arrow represents the starting point.
- The head indicates the direction.
- Choosing an appropriate scale is necessary before drawing any vector on paper.

## What Students Should Remember
Pro tip: In written exams, always put an arrow over variables like velocity ($\vec{v}$) or force ($\vec{F}$) to distinguish them from scalar magnitudes.
